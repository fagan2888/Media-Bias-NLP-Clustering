When Washington's Corcoran Gallery of Art, then the capital's only serious art collection, opened its Beaux-Arts building in 1897, its stone facade was boldly engraved with the names of 10 artists. The procession starts with Phidias, continues through Michelangelo, Rembrandt and Rubens, and ends with Ingres. These European figures then stood for the ideals of Western art; no actual paintings or sculpture by these Old Masters could be found inside.
