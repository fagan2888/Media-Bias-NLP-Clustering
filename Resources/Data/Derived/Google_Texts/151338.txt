SIX YEARS AFTER THE SECOND WORLD WAR, the probability of a third seemed high and rising. The Soviets had the bomb; Europe was riddled with tripwires. Yugoslavia was a major concern. Its Communist government had broken with the Soviet bloc three years earlier and relations were worsening. Would the Soviets invade? In March 1951, information generated by American intelligence agencies was gathered and distilled.
