Eurozone banks are making little headway cleaning up their balance sheets.

A year on from a eurozone bank “stress test” aimed at breathing life into the bloc’s lenders, €826 billion ($909 billion) of bad loans continue to fester on their balance sheets, down just 2% in the past 12 months, according to research conducted by law firm Linklaters LLP.
