Newly Finalized ACA Rules Include $250K Fine For False Info

Law360, Los Angeles (May 16, 2014, 8:26 PM ET) -- The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services on Friday finalized a set of rules proposed in March that set new parameters for the enforcement of the Affordable Care Act that will kick in next year, including potential $250,000 penalties for applicants who lie on insurance forms.



The new rules standardize some processes and streamline others, including an exceptions process for prescription drugs so customers with urgent medical issues have quicker access to drugs not covered by their plan, the CMS noted.
