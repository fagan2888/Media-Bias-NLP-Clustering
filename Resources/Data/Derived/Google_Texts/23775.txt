Tuberculosis is no longer on the decline in the U.S., after nearly a quarter century of steady reductions in cases of the deadly airborne disease, according to federal data released Thursday.

The number of TB cases rose last year for the first time in 23 years, and the rate of cases has leveled off at three per 100,000 people since 2013, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention said in a report.
