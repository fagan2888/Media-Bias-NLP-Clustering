Whenever a new production of a familiar and well-loved work of art is billed as “reimagined,” you’re usually right to suspect dirty work at the creative crossroads. That’s what’s happened to “Gigi,” Heidi Thomas’s rewrite of the failed 1973 stage version of the 1958 Alan Jay Lerner-Frederick Loewe screen musical about a Parisian teenager whose family is training her to become a courtesan.
