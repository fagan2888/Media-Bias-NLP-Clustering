The storefront church destroyed Wednesday in the East Harlem explosion was a neighborhood mainstay appreciated for its outreach to low-income people in an area just beginning to gentrify.

The Spanish Christian Church, whose sanctuary was in the ground floor of 1644 Park Ave., hosted sidewalk bake sales and gave away food, said Migdalia Mojica, 41 years old, a former parishioner who attended the church for three years.
