KAKE News On Your Side: Information About Avian Flu

H5N1 was the first Avian Flu, dectected in geese living in China in 1996. That strain of Avian Flu lead to a human infection in Hong Kong in 1997. H5N1 became more widespread in 2003 and to date, has killed around 450 people worldwide.

The H5N2 Avian Flu is what was detected in a Kansans backyard flock of chickens and ducks in Leavenworth County in March. H5N2 has not been proven to spread to humans yet. Scientists have seen that it can be transmitted to pigs, which causes them to be concerned the virus could mutate and spread to humans.

The Kansas Secretary of Agriculture issued a stop movment order on June 9, 2015.

The stop movmement order is to stop the commingling of poulry and live birds at events. Those events include county fairs, the Kansas State Fair, swap meets, and bird markets and auctions. Anyone in violation of this stop movement order could face a $1000 fine and other penalties.
