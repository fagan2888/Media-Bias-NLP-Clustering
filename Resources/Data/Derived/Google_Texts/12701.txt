Cold temperatures will bring in the new year in Denver.

Lows will be from 2 to 6 degrees through Friday, according to Jim Kalina, meteorologist for the National Weather Service in Boulder.

“We’re going to be in the 20s through New Year’s Day,” Kalina said of the high temperatures.

The upside is that the temperatures will rise gradually each day.

The low temperature for Saturday will be 10 degrees. On Sunday, it will 15 degrees in the morning and 35 in the afternoon in time for the Denver Broncos game against the San Diego Chargers.

Temperatures will continue to rise on Monday when the Front Range will be around normal, with a low of 19 and a high of 42.

The normal temperatures for Jan. 4 is a low of 17 and a high of 43, Kalina said.

Scattered snow showers are forecast for the mountains, where low temperatures will range from zero to -10 degrees.

Taking into account wind chill, it will feel like -20 degrees, Kalina said.
