In the constitutional challenge to the Affordable Care Act, Chief Justice John Roberts held that requiring citizens to buy health insurance was beyond the power of Congress to regulate interstate commerce. Yet he provided the fifth vote to uphold the law. How? Justice Roberts adopted what he called a "saving construction" that converted what the statute termed an "individual responsibility requirement" that was enforced by a "penalty" into an option either to buy health insurance or pay a small tax.
