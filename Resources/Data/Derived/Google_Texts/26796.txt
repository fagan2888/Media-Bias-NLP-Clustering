ANI Lil General Owen Anderson, 9 (front) tries on his new LSUA Generals baseball cap. With Owen are LSUA Generals Christian Cale (23, back left); Tyler Mathis (28, second from left); Seth Roy, student assistant coach and Stefan Porche. Owen was chosen as the "Lil General" to throw out the first pitch at the LSUA baseball game against Culver Stockton College. Owen suffered from a rare autoimmune condition similar to MS after catching a virus. After three years, he is able to talk and walk again. He recently returned to school. The child chosen as a "Lil General" is a child who has undergone a significant health condition. Both the child and team benefit from the experience."This is a dream come true for him," said his mother Hollie Anderson. Owen is a sports fan. He is a patient at Christus Cabrini Pediatric Therapy Center.-Melinda Martinez/mmartinez@thetowntalk.com The Town Talk Gannett Melinda Martinez/mmartinez@thetowntalk.com
