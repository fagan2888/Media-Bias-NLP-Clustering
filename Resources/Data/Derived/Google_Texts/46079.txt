A Rome woman was arrested Saturday for allegedly going on a stealing spree with bad checks in the Town and Village of New Hartford.



Police say Mary Snow-Titus, of Rome, stole $5,000 worth of merchandise from several stores throughout the town and village by writing nine checks on a closed bank account.



The 37-year-old faces nine counts of issuing bad checks, a misdemeanor and one count of grand larceny and scheme to a defraud, both felonies.



Snow-Titus was arraigned at the Town of New Hartford court and sent to the Oneida County Jail on $3,000 cash or $6,000 bond.
