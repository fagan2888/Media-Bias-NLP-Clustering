With “America Ascendant,” the widely respected Democratic pollster Stanley Greenberg has written an ambitious book, part history, part social analysis, part campaign memo. Along the way, he provides an interesting diagnosis of America’s ills. But his policy prescriptions, like those of politicians of both parties, don’t seem capable of achieving a cure.
