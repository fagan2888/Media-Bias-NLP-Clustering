CMS Home Health Model Follows Quality-Based Medicare Shift

Law360, New York (August 19, 2015, 12:06 PM ET) -- On July 10, 2015, the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services published in the Federal Register the calendar year 2016 Home Health Prospective Payment System proposed rule effective for episodes ending on or after Jan. 1, 2016.[1] CMS estimates that the net impact of the proposed rule would result in a 1.8 percent decrease in Medicare payments to home health agencies ($350 million decrease) for calendar year 2016 as provided at Table 1 of the proposed rule and on its home health agency website available here....
