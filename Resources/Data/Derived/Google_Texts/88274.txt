Sen. Ted Cruz (R-Tex.) broke an unspoken rule of etiquette on the Senate floor Friday when he called Majority Leader Mitch McConnell (R-Ky.) a liar as the chamber worked on a must-pass transportation bill.

Cruz’s outburst took place after McConnell set up votes on two controversial amendments — repeal of the Affordable Care Act and reauthorization of the Export-Import Bank — in a way that would make it difficult for other amendments to receive votes.
