In 1963, Martin Luther King Jr. called America to rise from the "dark and desolate valley of segregation." Over half a century after his most famous speech, Texas is creating a new kind of segregation and pushing us farther from King's dream. With the majority of uninsured Texans being people of color, Texas' refusal to provide access to health care for our state's poorest adults is worsening racial health inequalities. Texas legislators are choosing to keep our health care system separate and unequal.
