In the United States, the public debate over same-sex marriage goes back at least as far as 1970, when two male students at the University of Minnesota attempted to obtain a marriage license. In 1974, PBS aired a groundbreaking debate on the subject featuring Frank Kameny, the first person to legally challenge his firing from a government job due to sexual orientation.
