It just so happens that right after I finished David S. Shields’s substantial history of Lowcountry cuisine (that is, the Carolinas and Georgia), I gave talks to the South Carolina Upstate Mycological Society and the Mushroom Club of Georgia, in Atlanta. Usually speakers at mushroom clubs are put up in private homes, which means you have access to local folks and their cars and their knowledge of area restaurants. I had read about landrace benne seeds, Carolina Gold rice, red peas and other heritage Lowcountry foods and their emergence on the restaurant scene in “Southern Provisions,” and I was hot to try them.
