The Federal Reserve has been trying to prevent deflation, writes George Melloan, but the federal government has been “engineering its cause, excessive debt.” And it’s not just our government. Global debt of all types has soared above 300% of GDP, notes Mr. Melloan. Near-zero interest rates not only enabled government spending but also “encouraged consumers and business to releverage. Cars are now financed with low or no-interest five-year loans. With the 2008 housing debacle forgotten, easier mortgage terms have made a comeback.
