After the Supreme Court’s just-concluded term, many of the unfortunate souls who study the Constitution and write about the law for a living are wondering whether they should look for a new vocation. The jurisprudence on display in the Obergefell gay-marriage case is little more than mawkish “dignity” burble—contemptuous of the political process and glibly dismissive of concerns over the free exercise of religion. And it transpired yet again that in big and important cases, legal canons yield readily to judicial statism.
