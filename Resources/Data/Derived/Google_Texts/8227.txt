The Supreme Court declined Monday to rule on religious groups' challenge to ObamaCare's contraceptive mandate, instead throwing out previous rulings in favor of the Obama administration and sending the case back down to lower courts. The surprise move is a good sign for the groups, which were dissatisfied with the Obama administration's workaround to providing contraceptive care to employees of companies that object to the health-care law's mandate on faith-based grounds. They argued that filling out the letter objecting to contraceptive coverage goes against their faith, as it allows employees to receive contraceptive coverage from insurers.
