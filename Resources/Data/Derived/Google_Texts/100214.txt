The defending national champion University of Denver men’s lacrosse team won its 17th consecutive game over two seasons Sunday, routing Dartmouth 16-6 to capture the Faceoff Classic title with a 2-0 record.

A sellout crowd of 2,631 filled Peter Barton Lacrosse Stadium and watched the Pioneers dominate the Big Green (0-3). DU (4-0) won its last 13 games of the 2015 season while earning its first NCAA title in the sport.

DU junior captain Tyler Pace was selected MVP of the Face- off Classic after scoring three goals and adding three assists for a career-high six points Sunday. Pace scored 1 minute, 34 seconds into the game, giving the Pioneers the lead for good.

Zach Miller, Brendan Bomberry and Connor Cannizzaro each scored two goals for the Pioneers.

Seven players tallied a goal apiece for DU, including three from Colorado: Colton Jackson of Rock Canyon High School and Arapahoe graduates Bryce Parietti and Jack Bobzien.

Colin Woolford, Connor Donahue, Max Planning and Nate Marano also scored for DU.

DU goalies Alex Ready, Joey Gigantiello and David Winsor of Boulder combined to make 11 saves.

DU’s next game is Saturday at North Carolina.

Air Force (3-2) pinned a 13-10 loss on Sacred Heart (2-3) in the other Faceoff Classic game Sunday.
