The U.S. House of Representatives' Natural Resources Committee announced on Tuesday that it was canceling a law-writing session, known as a 'markup', that had been scheduled to begin Wednesday and go into Thursday.

But later on Tuesday, Congressional officials said the markup was scheduled for 5 p.m. EDT Wednesday, an hour postponement.

Republicans who control Congress have struggled to write legislation that would help Puerto Rico face roughly $70 billion of debt and a 45 percent poverty rate.

The most fiscally-conservative Republicans in Congress, though, have said they would not vote for legislation that did not have the Caribbean island squarely face its debt.
