Industries can be turned upside-down in an instant. It happened when Amazon and eBay transformed traditional retailing, and again when Netflix toppled Blockbuster's dominance for watching movies. The healthcare industry is not immune to this kind of disruption. Paul Keckley, Managing Director of the Navigant Center for Healthcare Research and Policy Analysis, has identified five areas in healthcare that are susceptible to dramatic change: drug costs, rural hospitals, academic medical centers, private insurers and physicians.
