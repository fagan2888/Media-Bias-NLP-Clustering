Two earthquakes, one a magnitude 4.0, rattled south-central Kansas on Saturday.

According to the U.S. Geological Survey, the first quake occurred just before 10:15 a.m. The magnitude 3.3 was centered about 17.3 miles southwest of Pratt and had a depth of 5.2 miles.

The larger of the two earthquakes, a 4.0 temblor, shook the same area at 1:45 p.m. It had a depth of 3.1 miles. It was originally reported as a 4.6.

Numerous KAKE views called to report feeling both quakes.

So far, no damage or injuries have been reported.
