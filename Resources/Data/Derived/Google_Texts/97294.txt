Standard & Poor's on Friday said it upgraded Ireland's government bonds by one notch to A-minus, saying the country is making good progress in getting a grip on its six-year banking-debt crisis.

It marks the first major upgrade of Ireland by S&P after it downgraded the country at the height of the economic ills, in 2011.
