Virginia Natural Heritage Program

What: 61 natural area preserves across the state, comprising 55,363 acres; 10 of them are located in the mountain region, including several in the New River Valley

Budget: $2.5 million; $1.3 million of that directly supports the natural area preserves

Employees: 40

History: Established by a 1989 act of the General Assembly to protect rare and fragile ecosystems and species, based on methods developed by the Nature Conservancy in the 1970s.

More information: Find descriptions of invasive species and native substitutes at www.invasivespeciesinfo.gov, or read more about Buffalo Mountain and other Virginia Natural Area Preserves at www.dcr.virginia.gov/natural_heritage/natural_area_preserves/
