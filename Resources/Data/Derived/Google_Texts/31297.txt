One walked out elated, convinced his life had just changed in a profound and remarkable way, so happy he had tears in his eyes and wanted to "run outside naked."

The other had tears in her eyes, too. But they were tears of frustration. Frustration that she was still — despite the promises of Obamacare — struggling to find a health care plan she could afford.

The two customers were the last of the day inside a mobile outreach van set up in West Long Branch Monday night as the enrollment deadline for the Affordable Care Act neared.

The scene that played out between 11:30 p.m. and midnight summed up the uncertainty over whether the act will improve life for the millions of previously — and still — uninsured. One found it a disaster. The other found it a godsend.
