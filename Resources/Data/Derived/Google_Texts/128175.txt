Multiple grass fires have broken out in south central Kansas Saturday afternoon, some threatening or damaging homes and property. Much of KAKEland remains under a Red Flag Warning until 6 p.m.

The National Weather Service Red Flag Warning remains in effect until 6 p.m. for multiple counties including Sedgwick, Harvey, Reno, Butler, Kingman, Sumner, Cowley, McPherson, Marion, Chase, Greenwood, Woodson, Harper, Elk, Chautauqua, Russell, Lincoln, Rice, Saline and Ellsworth counties.

The Red Flag warning denotes extreme grassland fire danger with winds in excess of 20 miles per hour, and low humidity.

The NWS warns any fire that develop are likely to spread rapidly, becoming very difficult to control. Outdoor burning is not recommended.
