Two men were arrested for offenses related to trafficking cocaine.

According to the Lenoir County Sheriff's Office, Samuel Alvares and Miguel Goicochea-Medina were arrested on January 23.

Investigators seized one kilogram of powder cocaine. They say the cocaine has a street value of $80,000 to $100,000.

The Lenoir County Sheriff's Office, State Bureau of Investigation, Kinston Department of Public Safety, and NC State Highway Patrol Aircraft Operations conducted the investigation.

Alvares and Goicochea-Medina are charged with trafficking cocaine by possession, trafficking cocaine by delivery, conspiring to traffic cocaine, and maintaining a vehicle for controlled substances.

Both men are in the W.E. "Billy" Smith Detention Center under 1 million dollar secure bonds.
