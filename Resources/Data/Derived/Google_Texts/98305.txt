At a press conference on Tuesday centering around the current government shutdown/default crises, President Obama took questions from seemingly every reporter in the room, except for the five national TV reporters. On Wednesday, the President bypassed the national television media again, instead taking his message to local audiences in four East Coast markets, as he granted interviews to Philadelphia news anchor Rick Williams, Tampa’s Keith Cate, Washington DC’s Doreen Gentzler, and Virginia’s Tracy Sears.

Amid some pretty clear indications that the White House is unhappy with the way some national TV outlets have covered the government shutdown, the President gave interviews to four local TV outlets Wednesday to discuss the effects of the shutdown, and to place added pressure on Republicans to resolve the continuing budget resolution and raise the debt ceiling, without making political demands.
