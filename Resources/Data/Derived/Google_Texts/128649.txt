In House Speaker Paul Ryan’s office, the Labor Department’s proposed fiduciary rule has been dubbed “Obamacare for financial planning.” Writing on the Ryan blog, Deputy Communications Director Julia Slingsby derided the rule, designed to ensure retirement advisers put clients’ best interests before their own profits, with a comparison to President Barack Obama’s Affordable Care Act. As the rule nears final approval, perhaps later this month, critics have grown louder, arguing the tighter rule would push up compliance costs, prompt higher fees and lead to advisers dropping middle-class clients.
