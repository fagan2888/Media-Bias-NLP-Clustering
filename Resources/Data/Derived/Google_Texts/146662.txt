BlackRock, the world's largest asset manager, in August acquired FutureAdvisor, which provides the technology to banks, brokerage firms, insurers and 401(k) plans.

Robo-advisers allow the companies to use algorithms to manage money for clients at a lower cost than with human advisers.

Leading robo-adviser providers include Betterment, Wealthfront and discount brokerage Charles Schwab, which are competing to use digital platforms to attract investors who may not meet fund managers' minimum asset requirements for human advice. BlackRock Chief Executive Larry Fink said in August he believed every major asset manager would eventually need to offer a robo-advice product.
