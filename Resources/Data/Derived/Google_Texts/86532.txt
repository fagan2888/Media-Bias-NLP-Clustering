The Colorado Avalanche Information Center issued an avalanche warning Saturday for the Front Range and Vail-Summit forecast zones.

The warning is effective from noon Saturday until 6 p.m. Sunday.

The center says the danger is “high” (4 on a scale of 5) near treeline and above treeline. The danger is “considerable” — a 3 — below timberline.

“Very strong westerly winds drifted snow near and above treeline,” the center said in its warning. “Avalanche conditions are very dangerous.Natural and human triggered avalanches are likely.Some avalanches may be very large and destructive.Backcountry travelers are advised to stay well clear of all avalanche terrain.”

The center said to avoid traveling on or under steep slopes.
