House Unveils Spending Bill At Odds With Obama's Goals

Law360, New York (June 10, 2015, 1:20 PM ET) -- Republicans in the U.S. House of Representatives on Wednesday unveiled a $20.2 billion fiscal 2016 financial services spending bill that is nearly $5 billion less than requested by the White House, would slash the IRS budget and change the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau’s funding mechanism.
