The early results are in from Saturday's opening day of pheasant season in South Dakota.

According to Game, Fish and Parks, hunters averaged 1.5 birds each in central South Dakota, while averaging 2-2.5 birds each in Hughes, Sully, Potter and Stanley counties.

In northeast South Dakota, Spink County reported hunters averaging 2 birds each.

While hunters averaged 1 bird each in McPherson, Faulk and Edmunds counties.

Southeast South Dakota hunters averaged 1-1.5 birds each in Beadle, Aurora, Hutchinson and Bon Homme counties.

In Western South Dakota, hunters averaged 1.5 birds each in Bennett and Perkins counties. Hunters were limited out in Ziebach County, with 3 birds per hunter.

Pheasant hunting season runs through January 4.
