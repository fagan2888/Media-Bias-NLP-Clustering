Joe Ducharme honored for 25-year career



MANCHESTER — Joseph M. Ducharme, Jr., P.E., has been selected the 2016 New Hampshire Engineer of the Year.



The award will be presented Thursday at the annual Engineers’ Week banquet at the SERESC Center in Bedford. The New Hampshire Society of Professional Engineers nominated Ducharme based on his record of professional accomplishments, service to his profession, record of contributions to his community and dedication to his family.

..

Ducharme is a licensed engineer in New Hampshire and a graduate of Texas A&M University where he attended the College of Civil Engineering, receiving his bachelor of science degree in 1986 and his master of engineering degree with a public works engineering focus in 1987.

..

He maintains active memberships in many professional associations, having served in a number of committees and boards of directors over his career. Ducharme is currently serving as treasurer for the American Council of Engineering Companies’ New Hampshire section, having served on the board of directors since 2010, and also as ACEC-NH president from 2012-14.

..

Ducharme’s career as a civil and environmental engineer spans 25 years focused primarily on water quality and municipal infrastructure projects. In all of his endeavors, from serving clients to serving his profession, Ducharme brings his friendly personality, thoughtfulness, tireless work ethic, patience, sense of humor, considerable experience and technical knowledge to every project.

..

At CMA Engineers, Ducharme’s roles include project manager and senior project engineer on a wide variety of civil and environmental engineering projects. He is active in the administration of the firm, serving on the board of directors, and as manager of the firms’ Manchester office. He is responsible for project management, senior project engineering, client development, contract negotiation, managing sub-consultants and construction administration.

..

Ducharme and his wife, Karen, have three daughters and one granddaughter. They live in Concord and are active in their community annually supporting their church as well as volunteering their time to a number of local, state and national charities.
