In his “ObamaCare’s Threat to Private Practice” (op-ed, Dec. 8), Dr. Scott Gottlieb likely assumes that new physicians want private practice. They don’t. Their need to pay down high educational debt with high starting compensation and their desire for work-life balance preclude private practice as a career choice. Solo and small-group practice is already dead as a result of these factors, dis-economies of scale and the continuing transition from volume to value-based payment.
