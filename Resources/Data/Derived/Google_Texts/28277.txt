Unable to bear children, wealthy Vermont couple Judy and Walt Ribke—among the few members of Homo sapiens in Colin McAdam's novel "A Beautiful Truth" (Soho, 292 pages, $25)—buy a chimpanzee named Looee from a circus employee. The Ribkes devote themselves lovingly to his care, as they would with a particularly needy son. He is, as a neighbor puts it, "neither wild nor owned"—he lives in his own customized house, dresses himself, eats with utensils, watches television.
