At 97 degrees Fahrenheit, the hottest temperature reached this year at the Denver International Airport was recorded Sunday afternoon, according to the National Weather Service.

Just before 3 p.m. Sunday the temperature hit 97 but fell to 90 within an hour as clouds rolled into the area.

There is a slight chance of storms for the rest of the Sunday afternoon into the evening hours for the Denver metro area, said meteorologist Bob Kleyla from the National Weather Service’s Boulder office.

Temperature at first pitch for the Rockies’ 2:10 p.m. home game was 94 degrees, the hottest of the season at Coors Field.
