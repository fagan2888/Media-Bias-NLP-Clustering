Longmont Police issued two 16-year-old males curfew tickets at about 11:50 p.m. Thursday in the 2300 block of Clover Basin Drive. This case was cleared by arrest.

At about 4:20 p.m. on Wednesday, Longmont police officers investigated a report of criminal mischief in the 1200 block of Kimbark Street. Several residents reported windows to their vehicles had been damaged by an unknown suspect. It appears the damage was done by an airsoft gun.

The Longmont Police Department on July 17 assisted the Routt County Sheriff's Office with an investigation in their jurisdiction. All further inquiries need to be directed to Routt County Sheriff's Office.

A burglary was investigated at about 10 p.m. Thursday by Longmont police in the 100 block of E. 4th Avenue. A 20-year-old male suspect had unlawfully entered the residence of a 24-year-old victim. After completing an investigation, the officers were informed by the victim that he did not wish to pursue charges. The case is closed.

At about 7:30 p.m. Thursday, Longmont police were dispatched to contact a party by phone reference lost property. Police contacted an adult female. The case is closed.
