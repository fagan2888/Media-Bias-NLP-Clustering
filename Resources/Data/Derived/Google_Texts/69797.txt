Somerset County Freeholder Deputy Director Patricia Walsh attended the annual luncheon honoring Somerset County centenarians on Wednesday at the Regency Jewish Heritage Nursing and Rehabilitation Center on DeMott Lane.

Sixteen honorees were in attendance: Philip Annich, 103, Norma Horger, 102, Reubin Lansky, 100, Jane Lee, 100, and Catherine Helen Stevens, 104, from Bridgewater, Margaret MacKenzie, 108, and Mildred Van Dyke, 102, from the Basking Ridge section of Bernards, Hannah Abse, 100, Dorothy Black, 101, Anna Girandola, 101, Sylvia Morrett, 102, Mollie Reuben, 102, and Emma Tyukody, 101, from the Somerset section of Franklin, Gloria Andersen, 100, of Hillsborough, Rita Ireland, 100, of Manville, Tessie Palatini, 101, of Somerville and Angelina Sangregorio, 105, of Watchung.

Honored in absentia were Edith Sumerfield, 100, of Bedminster, Michael LaBrunda, 100, and Anna Catherine Wachowicz, 100, of Basking Ridge; George Rother, 103, of Bridgewater, Jean Roth, 101, of Somerset and Claudia Beitz, 100, Carolyn McCullough, 101, of Somerville and Carolyn Dixon, 101, of Belle Mead.

The Somerset County Board of Chosen Freeholders participates each year in the nationwide celebration of Older Americans Month. Established in 1963, Older Americans Month recognizes the contributions of individuals age 60 and above who enrich and strengthen our nation's communities.
