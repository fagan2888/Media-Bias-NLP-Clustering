The wet spring continued with heavy rain Sunday evening causing isolated street flooding around metro Denver.

National Weather Service rainfall data show 1.54 inches fell near Interstate 70 and Interstate 225.

The rainfall has varied with .94 inches recorded in east-central Denver and 1.02 inches near the Rocky Mountain Arsenal National Wildlife Refuge at the northern edge of the city.

Less rain fell in south metro Denver.

NWS meteorologist Kyle Fredin said drier air should prevail Monday and Tuesday.

No tornadoes were reported.

A weakening line of rain showers and thunderstorms slowly was sweeping away from metro Denver to the eastern plains. One-inch hail was reported in the eastern part of the state.
