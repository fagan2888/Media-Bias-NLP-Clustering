EEOC Seeks Injunction To Stop Honeywell Biometric Testing

Law360, New York (October 28, 2014, 6:11 PM ET) -- The U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission petitioned a Minnesota federal judge on Tuesday to block Honeywell International Inc. from forcing employees to undergo biometric testing or risk facing penalties and reduced company contributions to an employer-sponsored health plan.



In a request for a temporary restraining order and preliminary injunction, the EEOC urged U.S. District Judge Ann D. Montgomery for an order blocking Honeywell from penalizing any employee who refuses to participate in biometric screening.
