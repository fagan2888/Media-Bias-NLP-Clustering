Yemen burns. ISIS is gaining ground in Iraq and Syria, and Iran continues to test the West with dangerous naval provocations designed to see who will flinch first.

It should be the perfect time to talk about a region in crisis. However, all but two heads of state declined President Barack Obama's invitation to come to Washington on Wednesday before retreating to Camp David on Thursday for what was supposed to be a round of meetings between top U.S. officials and leaders of the six nations that form the Gulf Cooperation Council. Bahrain, Oman and the United Arab Emirates are among the member states only sending deputies to join the secretary-general of the GCC, the emirs of Kuwait and Qatar, and Obama's national security team.
