Tenet Healthcare Corp. swung to a first-quarter profit as the hospital operator’s revenue benefited from increased admissions and more insured patients.

Tenet in March reached a deal that will give it control of United Surgical Partners International Inc. amid a wave of consolidation in the sector. The deal will create a joint venture with Welsh Carson Anderson & Stowe, combining Tenet’s short-stay, or ambulatory, surgery centers and imaging facilities with USPI, which the private-equity firm owns.
