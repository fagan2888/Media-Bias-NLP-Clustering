The Indiana Department of Transportation will close the eastbound right lane of Interstate 64 near Georgetown, Ind. Sunday and Monday for road repairs.

The lanes will be closed until 5 a.m. Monday for construction crews to saw cut pavement, remove failing roadbed and make full-depth concrete patches, according to an INDOT news release.

This weekend marks the beginning of closures to complete a $7.2 million repair and resurfacing project on I-64 between State Route 62 near Georgetown and the Sherman Minton Bridge.

Construction will occur every weekend- except during Thunder Over Louisville and Derby weekend- from 9 a.m. Sunday to 4 a.m. Monda, weather permitting.
