Blue Cross and Blue Shield

CHAPEL HILL, N.C. (WNCN) – Blue Cross and Blue Shield of North Carolina says it has resolved most of the issues involving bank overdrafts from customers.

On Monday, Blue Cross began airing radio ads to apologize to customers after about 3,200 incorrect amount deducted from their accounts.

Lew Borman, spokesman for Blue Cross and Blue Shield of North Carolina, said 2,600 of the 3,200 situations were resolved by Monday night.

Many customers took to social media to complain when they initially realized the overdrafts.

On the radio, the CEO of the company, Brad Wilson, said, “These difficulties are unacceptable and we apologize. To improve this situation, we are working around the clock—bringing in more staff and expanding customer service hours.”

Blue Cross said call volumes have been as much as six times the normal high. The company points to changing deadlines for the Affordable Care Act and a transition to a new customer service system for the problems.
