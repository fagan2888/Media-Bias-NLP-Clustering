What Americans really think of each other

A new public opinion survey of Americans' attitudes toward the major regions of the country reveals that we generally like each other, and associate positive qualities with the four major regions of the country: The Northeast, South, Midwest, and West. But there is one huge exception, one region that Americans, when shown a list of 20 adjectives, choose negative ones.
