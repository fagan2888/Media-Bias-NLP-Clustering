'Human Empire" is a phrase Rosalind Williams takes from Francis Bacon's 1624 tale "New Atlantis." Bacon used it to refer to what we might call technology, knowledge and human dominion over nature. In "The Triumph of Human Empire: Verne, Morris and Stevenson at the End of the World," Ms. Williams finds that her three 19th-century authors recognized a sudden and vast expansion of human empire and regarded it with deep misgivings.
