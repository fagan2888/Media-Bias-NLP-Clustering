5th Circ. Nixes Religious Groups’ Contraception Challenge

Law360, Los Angeles (June 22, 2015, 10:13 PM ET) -- The Fifth Circuit on Monday reversed lower court decisions in consolidated appeals of suits brought by religious organizations challenging contraceptive requirements for health insurance plans, ruling that the groups didn’t prove how providing insurance covering contraceptives would hinder their religious freedom.



The government had appealed lower court rulings which held that the religious organizations did not have to provide insurance for contraceptives as mandated by the Affordable Care Act under a religious exemption known as the Religious Freedom Restoration Act.
