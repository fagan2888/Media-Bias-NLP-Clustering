WASHINGTON, DC -- On Wednesday, President Barack Obama will travel to Durant High School in Durant, Okla., where he will deliver remarks on expanding economic opportunity for communities across the country, including the Choctaw Nation of Oklahoma.

The arrival and departure of Air Force One in Denison is closed to the public.

After his remarks, Obama will depart Durant and travel to the Oklahoma City area, where he will remain overnight.

Obama's remarks at Durant High School are closed to the public. His remarks will also be streamed live at whitehouse.gov/live.

On Thursday, Obama will visit the El Reno Federal Correctional Institution outside of Oklahoma City, marking the first visit by a sitting president to a federal prison.

While there, Obama will meet with law enforcement officials and inmates and conduct an interview for a Vice documentary that will air in the fall about the realities of our criminal justice system.
