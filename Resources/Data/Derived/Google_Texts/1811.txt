For Medicare open enrollment: The state Aging and Long-Term Services Department will be offering free advice in two ways.

First, the Aging and Disability Resource Center will be hosting meetings across New Mexico to answer questions about Medicare open enrollment. Some meetings are walk-in, but others require an appointment. To see the full list of meetings, visit nmaging.state.nm.us, click on the “Special Events” tab and look for Medicare open enrollment.

The other way to get help with Medicare open enrollment is by calling the center toll-free Monday through Friday, excluding holidays, between 7:45 a.m. and 5 p.m. The resource center can be reached at 800-432-2080. Open enrollment ends Dec. 7.

For help with individual insurance: The State Office of the Superintendent Insurance and the New Mexico Health Insurance Exchange have a series of informational sessions in Deming, Las Cruces, Las Vegas, N.M., Hobbs, Carlsbad, Roswell, Portales and Clovis, Santa Fe and Albuquerque now through Oct. 29.

For information on the state health exchange: Call 855-99-NMHIX or visit www.bewellnm.com.

To file a complaint: Call the state Office of the Superintendent of Insurance during business hours at 827-3811 to report problems with managed-care insurance or network adequacy.
