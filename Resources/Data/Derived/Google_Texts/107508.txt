After the first day of her campaign swing through Iowa last week, Hillary Clinton was reportedly struck that no one had asked her about inequality. She shouldn’t have been surprised, and probably wasn’t. Recent opinion surveys show inequality well down the list of public concerns. In a February CBS News poll, for example, only 4% of Americans named income disparities as the most important problem facing the country. In March only 2% told Gallup that the income gap was at the top of their list.
