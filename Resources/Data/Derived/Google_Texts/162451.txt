Your July 11 editorial “The Unaffordable Care Act” mentions one of the main drivers of double-digit premium increases, but doesn’t expand on it. The law dictates that nobody can be charged more than three times the lowest premium, but 64-year-old males consume six times more health-care dollars than 21-year-old males. Logic dictates that premiums, then, will ultimately tend to be much higher at the low end than would be experienced in an efficient marketplace.
