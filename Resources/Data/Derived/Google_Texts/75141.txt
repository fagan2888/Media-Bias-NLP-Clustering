11th Circ. Bumps Off Humana FCA Suit Over Patient Perks

Law360, Los Angeles (January 16, 2015, 6:48 PM ET) -- The Eleventh Circuit shut down a Florida man’s bid to revive his False Claims Act suit accusing Humana Inc. and several holistic medical clinics of offering patients illegal incentives, finding Friday that the meat of his accusations had been public knowledge, not insider information.



A three-judge panel ruled that Marc Osheroff’s suit, which included allegations that clinics were providing free exercise classes, transportation and other perks, relied on public information that Osheroff wasn't the original source for, according to a decision handed down Friday.
