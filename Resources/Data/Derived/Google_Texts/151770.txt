Salvation Army's city commander said they are behind $150,000 in donations compared to last year at this time.

The fundraiser goal is $1.8 million to help with programs in Wichita and Sedgwick County.

Major Joseph Wheeler said, "We are noticing that costs of course are up. We already know that it's going to cost us $15,000 more just to provide the food assistance that we've normally provided in the past."

Wheeler said the Salvation Army is need of toys, clothes, and donations. They are also asking for more volunteers.
