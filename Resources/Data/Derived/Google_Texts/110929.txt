From the looks of the UT Southwestern archives, it took a short forever to take down the old St. Paul hospital on Bryan Street in 1968. Sunday morning’s adios took but a few seconds, erasing five decades’ worth of memories.

In time that corner of Harry Hines and Inwood will become “1.1 million square feet of new space,” says UT Southwestern spokesman Russell Rian, “including a high-tech Simulation Center for training students, residents, fellows, and faculty, along with expansion of outpatient facilities.” But until it was emptied and gutted to make way for its replacement — the $800 million, 12-story William P. Clements Jr. University Hospital — St. Paul University Hopsital was a landmark, at least to those with deep, profound connections to it.

A high school girlfriend reminds that her father was chief of staff there; a local radio legend notes that his daughter was born there; I spent many days there many years ago as a close friend and colleague recovered from a heart attack suffered at a strikingly young age.
