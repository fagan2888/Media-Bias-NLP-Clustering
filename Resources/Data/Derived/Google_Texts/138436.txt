Kenneth L. Davis's "Hospital Mergers Can Lower Costs and Improve Medical Care" (op-ed, Sept. 16) argues that hospital mergers offer a wide range of economic and social benefits and should be encouraged in the face of concerns that provider consolidation can curtail competition. Dr. Davis cites no evidence that hospital mergers lead to social good, and he would be hard pressed to do so. There is overwhelming evidence, however, that hospital mergers can lead to significant price increases and that quality of care improves with greater competition.
