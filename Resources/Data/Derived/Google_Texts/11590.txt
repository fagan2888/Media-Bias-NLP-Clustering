Boulder police on Sunday released a sketch of a man who sexually assaulted a 20-year-old woman in Boulder early Saturday near East Euclid Avenue and 30th Street.

The woman said she was walking home alone at about 2:45 a.m. when she was grabbed from behind, pushed to the ground and sexually assaulted, police said.

The man was last seen running toward 30th Street.

He was described as Hispanic, in his 20s, about 5 feet, 5 inches tall, with a medium build. He had short, dark, spiked hair.

He had a full mustache, long sideburns and wore wire-rimmed glasses, police said. He also was wearing a dark hooded sweat shirt with white lettering and carried a dark backpack.

Anyone with information is asked to call Boulder police dispatch at 303-441-3333 or Detective Jack Gardner at 303-441-1851. Those who would like to provide information anonymously can call Northern Colorado Crime Stoppers at 1-800-222-8477 or 1-800-444-3776, as well as online at crimes-hurt.com.

There is a reward of up to $1,000.
