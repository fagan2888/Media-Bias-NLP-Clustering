The owner, Hugh Fuller, 58, purchased this property in Clearwater, Fla., located on the Gulf Coast, for $5.5 million in 1996, according to public records. Including an additional guesthouse and the furnishings, Mr. Fuller said he spent closer to $7.5 million. The property, pictured at center, spans roughly 3.5 acres. Dorian Aerial & Architectural Photograph …

Built in 1915, the property has been dubbed 'Century Oaks,' because of the old-growth oak trees that line the property, Mr. Fuller said. Some trees on the property are several hundred years old, he said. Jim Sykes …

Mr. Fuller works in the transportation industry and owns car dealerships, he said. A waterfront view from the Mediterranean-style home is shown. Jim Sykes …

The main home measures more than 23,000 square feet, according to the listing agent, and the property includes two swimming pools, one of which is shown. Mr. Fuller says that the home is perched on a bluff, and offers waterfront views. Jim Sykes …

'It's great to watch fireworks on the Fourth of July' from the boardwalk, Mr. Fuller said. 'It's one of the prettiest views in the world,' he said. Jim Sykes …

Part of the foyer, with marble flooring and intricate crown moldings, is shown. Elsewhere, the home includes Brazilian cherry-wood floors. Jim Sykes …

The ceilings in parts of the home reach 25 feet, according to the listing agent. The home includes a library, music studio and ballroom. Jim Sykes …

The main home includes 10 bedrooms, 15 full bathrooms and nine half baths. The 5,000-square-foot guesthouse includes additional bedrooms. Mr. Fuller says he is selling the house because his children are grown and 'it's just a little much for one person.' Jim Sykes …

There is a tennis court, an outdoor basketball court, and, in addition to the pools, a spa tub that can fit '10 to 15 people,' Mr. Fuller said. Jim Sykes …
