For senior private equity executives looking to take a bit of money off the table, the options usually come down to a public listing, a stake sale, or, in some cases, both.

As we learned this week, Ares Management is edging ever closer to an initial public offering, but energy-focused EIG Global Energy Partners settled on the latter move, selling a minority stake of itself to Affiliated Managers Group

With that transaction EIG joins a number of firms that have sold part of their business to investors ranging from sovereign wealth funds, pension funds, insurers and even other private equity shops.
