N.J. POLITICS ROUNDUP A collection of political news from the Statehouse and around New Jersey

TRENTON — A new poll shows Gov. Chris Christie's approval rating has dipped slightly for the first time in months.

CHRISTIE VISITS CAMDEN, TALKS ABOUT WEIGHT LOSS

Christie visited Camden yet again on Wednesday, touting the county police's metro division.

While there, he clarified a recent report that said he told Republican donors that he lost 85 points in the wake of weight-loss surgery.

The potential 2016 presidential candidate also said he will keep busy as chairman of the Republican Governors Association.

CHRISTIE, CUOMO SECURITY MEMO

In response to an uptick in terrorist activity, Christie and New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo announced that they have signed a memorandum on security that will boost police and national guard presence on mass transit in both states.

RAISING THE GAS TAX?

State lawmakers said raising the gas tax is likely one way New Jersey will help pay for fixing it crumbling roads and bridges.

TESLA ZAPS MOTOR VEHICLE COMMISSION

Tesla Motors has filed a legal brief saying New Jersey’s Motor Vehicle Commission did not have the authority to stop the company from selling its electric cars at showrooms and state officials knew it.

MORAN ON OBAMACARE

Star-Ledger political columnist Tom Moran writes that Obamacare is scoring big in New Jersey.
