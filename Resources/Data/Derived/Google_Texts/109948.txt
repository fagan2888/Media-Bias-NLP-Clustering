ROUGH CUT - SUBTITLED (NO REPORTER NARRATION) STORY: The Russian diesel-electric submarine, Rostov-on-Don, arrived in the Crimean port of Sevastopol on Friday (December 25) as the latest addition to the Russian Navy. The submarine, built in St. Petersburg, is equipped with Kalibr cruise missiles which it can launch while under water. On the way from the Baltic to the Black Sea, the sub launched several missiles at Islamic State positions in Syria. The submarine will be based at the Russian naval facility in Sevastopol. It is the second submarine to join the Russian Black Sea Navy in the past three months. Four more similar submarines are scheduled to arrive there in the coming years.
