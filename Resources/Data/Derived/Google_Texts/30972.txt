It appears there is a tie for first baby of Colorado in 2014.

Brooklyn Nolasco was born at 12:03 a.m., Wednesday, at Avista Adventist Hospital in Louisville, the Longmont Times-Call reported.

She weighed 6 pounds, 11 ounces. Her parents are Louis and Melanie Nolasco.

Bo Elizabeth Sieck was born at Memorial Hospital North in Colorado Springs at the exact same time, The Gazette reported.

She weighed 6 pounds, 10 ounces. Her parents are Eden and Sonner Sieck of Rush.

Poudre Valley Hospital in Fort Collins delivered its first baby of 2014 at 1:50 a.m.

Poudre Valley Hospital delivered 2,397 babies in 2013, according to spokeswoman Jill Clark.

A baby girl was born at 3 a.m. at University of Colorado Hospital and twins were born at 4:41 a.m. at Swedish Medical Center, according to hospital officials.

A baby girl was born at 3 a.m. at Rosé Medical Center.

Alexander Carpenter was born at 4:28 a.m. at Medical Center of the Rockies in Loveland.

He was 7 pounds.

The first New Year’s baby at Denver Health Medical Center was born at 6:03 a.m.

There were 3,437 babies delivered University of Colorado Hospital in 2013 – more in one year than ever before, said Dan Weaver, hospital spokesman.
