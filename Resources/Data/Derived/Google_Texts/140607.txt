By: ICTMN Staff

Her quill and stained glass work is unique and imaginative. Using quills for decorating Native items goes back centuries, but stained glass is quite recent, yet putting them together is a perfect marriage.

Leonda Fast Buffalo Horse, who lives on the Blackfeet Reservation, has done just that. Her artful and skilled creations have paid the bills since 1994.

“I use road-killed porcupines and have to pull the quills out with all the hair. There can be 30,000 quills on a porcupine,” she said. “They are all different sizes and come out with hair, bugs and everything so I have to wash them and sort the hair out. That takes me about eight hours. Then you can dye them,” she explained. This amount of prep work helps explain why quills gave way to beads when they became available.
