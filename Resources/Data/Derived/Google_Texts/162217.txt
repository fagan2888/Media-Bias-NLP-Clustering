The law firm of Stoll Keenon Ogden has announced that longtime Managing Director Bill Lear is stepping down after 26 years in the firm’s top leadership role, in order to focus on his law practice and civic activities.

The firm has elected P. Douglas Barr as its fourth managing director since its founding in 1897. Lear will continue to serve the firm as chairman emeritus.

Since joining the firm in 1980, Lear has worked primarily in the business litigation and real estate and government relations law. His areas of expertise include equine litigation, eminent domain, real estate litigation and thoroughbred racing regulations.

P. Douglas Barr. (Photo: Stoll Keenon Ogden)

Lear will continue as a trustee of Keeneland and as vice chairman of The Jockey Club. This fall, he will begin a one-year term as chairman of the Kentucky Chamber of Commerce.

Barr joined the firm in 1986 and is a member of the business litigation and intellectual property practice groups. He also has headed the firm’s Client Service Committee.

Stoll Keenon Ogden has 140 attorneys, with offices in Louisville, Lexington and Frankfort, as well as in Evansville, Ind., and in Greater Pittsburgh, Pa.
