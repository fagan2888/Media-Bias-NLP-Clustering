Tuesday

• U.S. Rep. Jackie Walorski, R-Jimtown, was appointed to a special conference committee charged with negotiating the final details of comprehensive legislation to curb heroin and opioid abuse.

Wednesday

• In a white paper, U.S. Sen. Joe Donnelly, D-Ind., presented a series of policy recommendations intended to prevent further offshoring of American jobs.

Generally speaking, the recommendations reward companies that invest in the U.S. and penalize those that offshore jobs to countries like Mexico or China.

“We have an obligation to try to … ensure that federal policies are designed to benefit the U.S. economy and … encourage investment in the foundation of our economy — American workers, their families, and communities,” Donnelly said in a statement.

• U.S. Sens. Dan Coats, R-Ind., and Joe Donnelly, D-Ind., expressed support for U.S. District Court nominee Winfield Ong during a Senate Judiciary Committee hearing.

Ong has been nominated to the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Indiana.

• In a floor speech, U.S. Sen. Dan Coats, R-Ind., blasted insurance premium increases for policies on the federal Health Insurance Marketplace in Indiana.

“Rather than making health care more affordable and accessible, Obamacare has actually driven up health care costs and decreased choice of doctors for too many American families and businesses,” Coats said. “It is long past time for repeal of (this) disastrous health care law.”

• U.S. Reps. Jackie Walorski, R-Jimtown, and Fred Upton, R-St. Joseph, voted in favor of legislation appropriating $622.1 million for Zika-related response programs.

“These funds are critical to efforts at home and abroad to control the spread of this devastating disease and develop effective medical treatments for those affected,” Walorski said in a statement.

• U.S. Rep. Jackie Walorski, R-Jimtown, helped pass the National Defense Authorization Act, which authorizes U.S. defense spending.

The act includes an amendment, offered by Walorski, requiring the National Security Agency to comply with the Freedom of Information Act.

“This NDAA makes critical investments in both the maintenance of existing equipment and the development of modern military platforms to maintain readiness amid growing challenges around the world,” Walorski said in a statement.

Thursday

• U.S. Sen. Dan Coats, R-Ind., expressed opposition to the Obama administration’s guidelines on transgender access to public school bathrooms.

“This announcement violates the Constitution and is based on politics, not what is best for our students,” Coats said in a statement. “Washington bureaucrats do not have the authority to dictate restroom policies at Indiana schools.”

• In a floor speech, U.S. Sen. Dan Coats, R-Ind., highlighted $7.7 billion in unused funds in the Treasury Department’s Home Affordable Modification Program.

“Congress could and should do more than continue allowing this program to sit on $7.7 billion,” Coats said. “Congress should rescind the remaining $7.7 billion in unused HAMP funds.”

Friday

• U.S. Sen. Dan Coats, R-Ind., responded to the latest Indiana employment report, which showed the state adding 12,000 new jobs in April.

The unemployment rate increased from 5 to 5.2 percent.

“Today’s report shows that Indiana’s private sector employment has increased to its highest level ever and labor force participation continues to outpace the national rate,” Coats said in a statement.

• U.S. Sen. Joe Donnelly, D-Ind., announced his support for the Veterans First Act, which aims to improve veteran care and reform the Veterans Choice Program.

“Veterans deserve access to timely and quality health care. The ‘Veterans First Act’ is important to improve the Choice Program and address the long wait times and senseless hurdles that our veterans continue to face,” Donnelly said in a statement.
