A Dallas Morning News story on Sunday explores the personal financial statements that most Dallas County elected officials must file each year to shed light on their potential conflicts of interests.

Part of the story focuses on the difficulty — for a variety of reasons — in tracking down these public records. So The News is posting the most recent financial disclosures it received for the 87 county-level officials who must file the forms.

Please see after the jump to view links to all the officials’ statements. Some information on the forms have been redacted. That includes home addresses, telephone numbers and the names of officials’ children.
