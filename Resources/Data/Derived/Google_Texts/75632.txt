Daughters’ hospitals aren’t alone in facing financial trouble

T he $843 million deal to sell the financially strapped Daughters of Charity Health System hospitals to the for-profit Prime Healthcare Services was always going to come with difficult trade-offs. Prime’s decision to back out of the deal last week, citing state Attorney General Kamala Harris’ “onerous and unprecedented” conditions for the sale, doesn’t change any of the underlying difficulties facing all hospital systems in the Bay Area.
