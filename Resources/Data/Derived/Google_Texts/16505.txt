During World War II, the Japanese army used hundreds of thousands of Asian civilians and Allied prisoners of war to build a supply line running 250 miles from present-day Thailand into Burma. The Death Railway, as it came to be called, was a graveyard for an unknown number of these forced laborers. "The Bridge on the River Kwai"—both Pierre Boulle's best-selling novel and the award-winning film—brought the brutal story to the public's attention.
