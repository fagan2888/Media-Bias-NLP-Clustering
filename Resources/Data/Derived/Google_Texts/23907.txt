HHS Watchdog Pressed On New Health Fraud Safe Harbors

Law360, New York (March 9, 2015, 7:37 PM ET) -- Health insurers, doctors and drugmakers are pushing the Office of Inspector General at the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services to exempt an array of emerging business practices from liability under the Anti-Kickback Statute, newly released correspondence shows.



The letters posted online Friday addressed the OIG’s annual solicitation of ideas for new safe harbors from the AKS, which restricts financial incentives that could influence treatment and reimbursement decisions.
