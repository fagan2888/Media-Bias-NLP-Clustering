Initial Trends

Trends at 9:20 IST on news channel NDTV show the Congress party is headed for a major defeat in Delhi where it has ruled for 15 years, and in Rajasthan where it has held power since 2008.

In Madhya Pradesh, the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party are in the lead. The contest in Chhattisgarh is still neck and neck between the BJP and Congress.

The biggest loss for Congress appears to be in Rajasthan, where initial trends show the BJP is leading in 72 seats. Congress is ahead in 21 seats in the state, suggesting it will be ousted from power in these polls, according to first-take trends.

Abhishek Manu Singhvi, spokesman for Congress nationally, said that “cyclical anti-incumbency” was responsible for Congress’s dismal performance in Rajasthan.

In Madhya Pradesh, trends on NDTV show that the BJP is leading in 58 seats while Congress is ahead in 21 seats.

According to NDTV, the BJP will almost certain to win in Delhi and Harsh Vardhan will become chief minister, unseating Sheila Dikshit who has held the post for three consecutive terms.

The BJP is leading in 22 seats in Delhi, the Congress in 8 seats.

The Aam Aadmi Party, the anti-corruption party, is set to make a resounding debut led by Arvind Kejriwal. It is leading in 18 seats in the capital.
