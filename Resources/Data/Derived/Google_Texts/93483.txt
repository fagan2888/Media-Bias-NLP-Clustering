Pima County Sheriff's deputies swarmed the area March 19 after inmate Sarah Herrera was discovered to be missing. (Source: Tucson News Now)

A 22-year-old woman serving time for misdemeanor trespassing and shoplifting walked away from a work crew around 7:30 a.m. on Saturday, March 19.

Sarah Herrera had served four days of an 18-day sentence when she walked away from Pima Animal Care Center.

MOBILE USERS: The KOLD News 13 mugshots of the month are available HERE.

She was wearing a red Pima County Jail uniform, possibly with a red sweatshirt. After her escape, a white SUV was seen parked in the area where jail clothing was found, according to a news release issued by the Pima County Sheriff's Department.

Herrera is African-American and 5-foot-6 and 100 pounds.

The PCSD Fugitive Investigative Strike Team – known as FIST – has taken over the investigation.

Anyone who knows where Herrera might be should call 911 or the 88-CRIME anonymous tip line.
