If Matteo Renzi becomes Italy's new prime minister, he will lead the country's 62nd government since World War II and the fourth in little more than two years. If he were to survive the rest of the legislature—due to end in 2018— he would be one of the longest-serving premiers Italy has ever seen.

But Italy's history is stacked up against him. The country's revolving-door governments have long made its political system a punch line...
