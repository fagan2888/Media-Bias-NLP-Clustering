The IUD is making a comeback more than 30 years after a generation of women was scared away from the birth-control device by the faulty Dalkon Shield.

This year, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration approved Bayer HealthCare Pharmaceuticals' Skyla, the first new intrauterine device on the market in 13 years. Planned Parenthood has launched a nationwide campaign in its clinics promoting a copper IUD as the most effective form of emergency contraception.
