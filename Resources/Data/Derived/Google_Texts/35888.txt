CMS Queried Over State ACA Exchanges Still Getting Fees

Law360, New York (December 10, 2015, 3:02 PM ET) -- Republican House committee leaders on Wednesday asked why the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services allows states that have since abandoned their own online insurance exchanges under the Affordable Care Act to continue collecting user fees.



Hawaii, Nevada, New Mexico and Oregon have all abandoned their own online exchanges and opted to use the federally facilitated marketplace, better known as HealthCare.gov, the committee leaders said in a Dec. 8 letter made public Wednesday.
