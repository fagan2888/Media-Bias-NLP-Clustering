Back in his day, George Morland (1763-1804) was a much sought-after British genre painter, but as can be gathered from a late portrait he painted of himself and his skinny assistant in a grimy attic, he wasn't a happy man. Growing up, his father had kept him copying Dutch and Flemish paintings round the clock, and he never shook off the habit of industrious work. Surrounding himself with a menagerie of guinea pigs, rabbits, dogs, pigeons and a donkey serving as models, Morland produced more than 4.000 paintings.
