Syracuse, N.Y. -- The federal government is awarding $3.48 million to 54 New York health centers, including four in Central New York, to recognize their achievements in providing high quality, comprehensive care.

The centers will use the federal Affordable Care Act funding to expand quality improvement systems and infrastructure, and improve primary care service delivery in the communities they serve.

Nationwide, $63.3 million is going to 1,153 health centers.

Of that amount, $209,766 is going to these Central New York health centers:

Syracuse Community Health Center, $72,945

East Hill Medical Inc., Auburn, $50,428

Family Health Network of Central New York, Cortland, $44,970

Northern Oswego County Health Services Inc., Pulaski, $41,423
