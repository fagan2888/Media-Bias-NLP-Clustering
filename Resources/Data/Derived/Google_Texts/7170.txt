Bill Gates and Paul Allen

Bill Gates was 19 and Paul Allen was 22 when they started Microsoft MSFT, +2.42% in 1977. Their software company would help usher in the PC revolution with the Windows operating system.

Gates, a former company chief executive, stepped down as chairman in February and is now an adviser to CEO Satya Nadella. He tops the latest list of Forbes’ magazine’s richest people and his Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation supports projects in education, in health and in the developing world.

Allen is also a philanthropist. His business interests include ownership of the NFL’s Seattle Seahawks, the NBA’s Portland Trail Blazers and a stake in the Seattle Sounders professional soccer team.
