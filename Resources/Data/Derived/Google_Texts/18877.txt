Within moments of the historic U.S. Supreme Court decision Thursday upholding the Affordable Care Act, supporters and opponents of Obamacare quickly took to social media voicing their opinion.

Speaking on the U.S. Senate floor minutes after Thursday's ruling to uphold a key portion of the law was announced, Minority Leader Harry Reid said, "Enough's enough. Let's move on."

MORE: What politicians, experts are saying about the ruling

Supreme Court Justice Antonin Scalia summarized from the bench his dissent to the ruling: "We should start calling this law SCOTUScare."

President Obama is set to make a Rose Garden appearance at the White House to talk about the decision this morning.
