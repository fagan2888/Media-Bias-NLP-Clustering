There are 19 states that have chosen not to expand their Medicaid programs even with federal financing assistance.

They are Alabama, Florida, Georgia, Idaho, Kansas, Maine, Mississippi, Missouri, Nebraska, North Carolina, Oklahoma, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Virginia, Wisconsin and Wyoming.

An expansion proposal is included in the state budget plans for the governors of South Dakota, Virginia and Wyoming.

The recently elected Republican governor of Kentucky, Matt Bevin, has notified federal health officials that he plans to shut down the health insurance exchange his state built to enroll residents for coverage under the Affordable Care Act.
