Some two-thirds into the Japanese film “Port of Flowers” (1943), big news hits the small island where the movie is set: Pearl Harbor has been attacked. The great actor Chishu Ryu delivers the announcement to the assembled villagers, eliciting a cry of “Banzai!” from Chieko Higashiyama, another screen stalwart of the period. (Fans of Japanese cinema may recognize them as the aged couple at the center of Yasujiro Ozu’s “Tokyo Story,” made 10 years later.) The scene moves this comedy, the first picture directed by Keisuke Kinoshita, into the realm of war propaganda, and history’s long shadow has added further...
