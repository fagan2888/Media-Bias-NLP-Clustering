In its first two years, the Affordable Care Act has helped enroll nearly half a million New Yorkers in private health insurance plans. In New York, and across the country, the new consumers, most of whom have help from federal subsidies to pay their premiums, have been a boon to insurers, which have seen their revenues grow and their stock prices soar.
