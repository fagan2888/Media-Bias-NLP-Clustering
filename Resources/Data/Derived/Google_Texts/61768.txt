Nike Inc. NKE, -0.27% shares rose in the extended session Tuesday after the athletic apparel and gear maker topped Wall Street estimates for quarterly earnings and said orders were higher than last year, but sales fell short of the consensus estimate. Nike shares advanced 2% to $134.50. The company reported fiscal second-quarter earnings of 90 cents a share on revenue of $7.69 billion. Analysts surveyed by FactSet had forecast earnings of 86 cents a share on revenue of $7.81 billion. Nike said that future orders for footwear and apparel from Dec. 2015 to April 2016 were 15% higher than they were for the year-ago period.
