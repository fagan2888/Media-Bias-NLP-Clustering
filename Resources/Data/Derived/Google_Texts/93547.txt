In Brief

• ASA Fitness is now offering private, one-on-one training for athletes in Northern Colorado without a long-term commitment. Contact: Ken Minifield, 231-2299, or email asafitness42@gmail.com.

• Woodward Inc. dba Industrial Products Group, of Fort Collins has won a $48,011.60 federal contract from the Defense Logistics Agency, Columbus, Ohio, for diesel engine governors.

• Tynan's Nissan will be working with the public/private agency Drive Electric Northern Colorado to discount Nissan Leaf automobiles through the rest of December. For more information, go to the website driveelectricnoco.org/group-buy/.

• The Haunted Game Cafe, 3307 S. College Ave., Fort Collins, raised more than $4,500, all of which went to Children's Hospital Colorado, through its Extra Life Gaming Marathon. It is part of a benefit to raise funds for Children's Miracle Network Hospitals across North America.

• Taco John's, 1635 N. Lincoln Ave., will make a donation to Project Healing Waters Fly Fishing Inc. for every Nachos Navidad ordered in the month of December.

• The national Salted Chef personalized chef service has launched service in Larimer County. According to a release from the company, a Salted Chef personal chefs will prepare meals of choice in subscribers' kitchens. On the Web: www.saltedchef.com/fortcollins.

• High Country Beverage has promoted Eric Franecki to become its Colorado craft brand manager and Shane Redman as its national craft brand manager.
