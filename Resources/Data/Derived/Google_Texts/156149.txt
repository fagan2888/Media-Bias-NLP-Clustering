A substitute teacher at Matawan-Aberdeen Regional High School was arrested Thursday night, accused of engaging in sexually explicit conversations with two minors.

Philip Riveley, 29, of Woodbridge, was arrested by detectives from the Monmouth County Prosecutor's Office and Matawan police, with help from the Woodbridge police. Riveley was charged with two counts of second-degree endangering the welfare of a child.

A joint investigation by the prosecutor's office Special Victims Bureau and Matawan police found Riveley engaged in sexual conversation with 15-year-old and 17-year-old female students at the school. If convicted, Riveley faces a sentence of five to 10 years in prison.

Riveley is being held in the Monmouth County jail in Freehold Township on a $150,000 full-cash bail.

Anyone with information can call the prosecutor's office Detective Andrea Tozzi at 1-800-533-7443 or Detective Joseph Lovallo of the Matawan Police Department at 732-566-1010.
