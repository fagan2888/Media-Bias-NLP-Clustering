Yields on government bonds from both sides of the Atlantic sank on Tuesday as persistent worries over the global growth outlook sent investors flocking to relatively safer assets to preserve capital.

The yield on the benchmark U.S. 10-year Treasury note dropped to 2.127%, the lowest closing level since Aug. 24, compared with 2.212% Monday. Yields fall as bond prices rise.
