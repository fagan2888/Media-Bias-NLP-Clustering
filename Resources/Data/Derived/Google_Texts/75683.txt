The high country is seeing some serious snow this weekend.

A low pressure system and its associated cold front continues to track across Arizona, bringing Valley showers, mountain snow and chilly temperatures along with it.

The National Weather Service of Flagstaff reported that as of 11 a.m. Saturday, Flagstaff had received 5 inches of snow from this storm and the Pinetop-Lakeside area had received more than 6 inches.

A winter weather advisory continues until 6 p.m. for the Mogollon Rim and White Mountains above 6,000 feet. Snow totals are expected to range from 2 to 6 inches along the Rim and 4 to 8 inches in the White Mountains by Saturday night.

Southern Gila County is also under a winter weather advisory above 5,000 feet until 5 p.m. One- to 3-inch snow totals are expected.

Drivers should anticipate winter driving conditions with areas of low visibility, slick roads and blowing snow.

Around noon, the Arizona Department of Transportation warned drivers to watch for hail along Interstate 10 to the east of Tucson. ADOT reported several crashes on I-10 between Vail and Benson due to hail and icy roads. Slush and ice was spotted on northbound Highway 87 south of Payson.
