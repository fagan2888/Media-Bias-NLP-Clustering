<p>Duck Duck Goose Consignment sale is celebrating its 10th birthday this year. The sale has grown from 36 consignors and two tents in a Knoxville backyard to seasonal sales in nine different cities. (Photo special to the News Sentinel)</p>

Fright-free family fun includes free movies, crafts, pumpkin decorating

Free movie on Saturdays

Watch a family friendly Halloween-themed movie for free every Saturday in October at the Halls Cinema 7. Screenings are at 10 a.m. and will include "Haunted Mansion" on Oct. 4; "Hotel Transylvania" on Oct. 11; "Monsters Inc." on Oct. 18; "Hocus Pocus" on Oct. 25. Seating is limited to the first 200, so arrive early. More: Halls Cinema 7, 3800 Neal Drive, 865-922-1417, http://www.hallscinema7.net

Creepy crafts

Make spooky crafts to take home at Michael's Kids Club. Classes start every half hour between 10 a.m.-noon and cost $2 each per child. The Halloween-themed projects include Painted Halloween rocks, Oct. 4; Mummy Madness, Oct. 11; Frankenstein Mask, Oct. 18; Halloween Fun treat bag craft and face-painting, Oct. 25. Decorate a kid's Halloween T-shirt for free between 1-4 p.m. Oct. 18 (T-shirt purchase necessary). More: 9622 Kingston Pike, 865-670-9096,http://www.michaels.com

Pumpkin patch kids

Trader Joe's will be opening their pumpkin patch on Oct. 11. Families can purchase and decorate their pumpkins between 1-3 p.m. on Oct. 11-12, 18-19, 25-26. The best creations will be judged and the winner will receive a goody bag. Children's favors will include stickers and suckers. There will be free face-painting for children, too. More: Trader Joe's, 8001 Kingston Pike, 865-670-4088

Haunted house

Build a haunted house at the Lowe's Build and Grow Clinic for kids. These free events are offered at all area Lowe's stores from 10-11 a.m. Oct. 11 and spaces fill up fast, so be sure to register for your local store now. More: http://lowesbuildandgrow.com/pages/fullschedule.aspx

Pumpkin party food

At the Williams Sonoma's free Junior Chef kids' classes this month, make pumpkin treats for Halloween parties at 10 a.m. Oct. 18 and decorate cookies with fun and spooky designs at 10 a.m. Oct. 25. Suitable for ages 5-13 years. More: West Town Mall, 7600 Kingston Pike, 865-539-1070, http://www.williams-sonoma.com/pages/store-events/store-events/junior-chef-classes.html

Free comic

Stop by The Comic Exchange between 10 a.m.-8 p.m. on Oct. 25 and pick out a free Halloween-themed comic book. There are 25-30 select books to choose from, and most are geared towards younger readers, such as Casper the Friendly Ghost and Wendy the Good Witch. More: 3711 Chapman Highway, 865-573-2280, http://www.halloweencomicfest.com/StoreLocator

Costume dress rehearsal

Kids can show off their Halloween costumes between 10 a.m.-3 p.m. Oct. 25 at Smart Toys and Books. The store will be giving out free treat bags and hosting a free Halloween craft. More: 9700 Kingston Pike, 865-691-1154, http://www.smarttoysandbooks.com

Dress up for Doughnuts

Krispy Kreme are giving away one free doughnut to anyone who comes into one of their stores in a costume on Halloween, Oct. 31. Be sure to try one of their Ghostbuster's-themed doughnuts. More: http://www.krispykreme.com

Upcoming Consignment Sales

Duck Duck Goose Consignment Sale founder Christy Tison and Courtney Ibach are celebrating 10 years in business with their upcoming fall and winter children's consignment event. The sale is scheduled for 10 a.m.-8 p.m. Oct. 1-4. On Oct. 3 is "Fundraiser Friday" ? any organization can go to www.dkdkgoose.com and printout the fundraiser form. Present the form at checkout and 10 percent of the purchase will go directly to that group. Shop thousands of children's and baby items and save even more during half-price day on Oct. 4. More: Knoxville Expo Center, 5441 Clinton Hwy, http://dkdkgoose.com/knoxville/

Westwood Children's Consignment sale is setting up at a new location at 7328 Middlebrook Pike in Suite D next to Gino's Pizza this season. Shop from 9 a.m.-4 p.m. Oct. 2-4; the half-price sale is 4-6 p.m. on Oct. 4. Find deals on fall and winter clothing in sizes newborn to juniors. The sales includes toys, books, games, DVDs, sports equipment, baby equipment, bedding, play items, small furniture and toddler booster seats. More: https://www.facebook.com/pages/Westwood-Childrens-Consignment-Sale/

Adopt to Consign sale event raises money to assist local families with the costs associated with adoption. Shoppers can access the pre-sale between 6-8 p.m. Oct. 2 when they make a $10 donation at the door. Regular hours are from 8 a.m.-3 p.m. Oct. 3 and 8 a.m.-2:30 p.m. Oct. 4. Most items half-price between 11:30 a.m.-2:30 p.m. Oct. 4. Expect to find gently used children's clothing in sizes newborn through teen size 16 suitable for all seasons. There will be shoes, costumes, formal wear, winter coats, accessories, maternity clothes, books, games, toys and baby gear. More: Laurel Church of Christ, 3457 Kingston Pike, 865-924-9885, https://www.facebook.com/Adopttoconsign
