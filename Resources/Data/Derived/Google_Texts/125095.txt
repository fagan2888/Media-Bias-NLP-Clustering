Frederick-Firestone Fire and Mountain View Fire Rescue responded to the scene of a house explosion on Cherryvale Street in Firestone at 11:13 a.m. today.

According to authorities, two people were working on the roof when an explosion happened on the second floor of the home, blowing out the windows on the second story.

Both of those workers were taken to a local hospital for treatment. The extent of their injuries is unavailable.

The homeowner was in the basement at the time of the explosion and was uninjured.

Three cats were able to escape the explosion, however the homeowner is still looking for a fourth cat.

The explosion did not trigger a fire. The source of the explosion was not immediately clear, but police believe it may be natural gas related.

Aaron Owens, senior manager of strategic communications for SourceGas said there was no gas leak outside the home and the cause of the explosion remains under investigation.

“We continue to work closely with local emergency responders and investigators,” he said.
