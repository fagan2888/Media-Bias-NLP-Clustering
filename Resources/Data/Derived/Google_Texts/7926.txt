 Amy Kautzman has been appointed the new dean of Sacramento States University Library effective Monday, March 2. Ms. Kautzman currently serves as the associate university librarian, Academic Services, at the University of California, Davis.

Prior to her current position, she was the associate university librarian, Humanities and Social Services, and acting head of Access Services at UC Davis. Before she joined the UC Davis Library, she served as department head for Research, Reference and Collections at the University Library at UC Berkeley.

She also has held senior librarian positions at Harvard College Library; Harvard University; Cambridge; and at the University Library, Northeastern College, Boston.

 Michael Maloney has been named chief operating officer effective immediately for AmeraMex International Inc. (OTC PINK: AMMX) of Chico, a seller and lessor of heavy equipment. Mr. Maloney jointed the company's board of directors in March 2013.

He will maintain his position of director of public safety education and training at Butte College in Oroville.

Prior to his position at Butte College, he had a 32-year career in law enforcement that culminated with his assignment as the chief of police in Chico. Concurrent with his law enforcement career, Mr. Maloney spent nearly 30 years as an educator at the Butte College Police Academy.

 Connie Gipson, 50, of Bakersfield, has been appointed associate director of general population male offenders at the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation, where she has served as acting associate director of general population male offenders since 2013. Ms. Gipson held several positions at California State Prison, Corcoran from 2010 to 2013, including warden, acting warden and chief deputy administrator.

This position requires Senate confirmation and the compensation is $142,548. Ms. Gipson is a Democrat.

 Deborah Hysen, 53, of Granite Bay, has been appointed director of the Division of Facility Planning, Construction, and Management at the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation, where she has served as acting director since 2014 and has held several positions since 2007, including deputy director and chief deputy secretary.

This position requires Senate confirmation and the compensation is $157,884. Ms. Hysen is a Republican.

 Jeffrey Macomber, 45, of Sacramento, has been appointed warden at California State Prison, Sacramento, where he has served as acting warden since 2014 and has held several positions since 2008, including chief deputy administrator and correctional administrator. He held several positions at the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation from 2004 to 2008 and from 1994 to 2000, including correctional administrator, captain, staff services manager and associate governmental programs analyst.

This position does not require Senate confirmation and the compensation is $135,828. Mr. Macomber is a Republican.

 Jerome Price, 57, of Turlock has been appointed warden at the Deuel Vocational Institution, where he has served as acting warden since 2012 and has held several positions since 2011, including chief deputy administrator and correctional administrator. He served in several positions at the Sierra Conservation Center from 2003 to 2011 and from 1991 to 1996, including correctional administrator, captain, lieutenant, correctional counselor and sergeant.

This position does not require Senate confirmation and the compensation is $135,828. Mr. Price is a Democrat.

 Stuart Sherman, 52, of Visalia, has been appointed warden at the California Substance Abuse Treatment Facility and State Prison, Corcoran, where he has served as acting warden since 2013 and has served in several positions since 2004, including chief deputy administrator and correctional administrator.

He held multiple positions at California State Prison, Corcoran from 1997 to 2004, including correctional administrator, captain and correctional counselor. Sherman was a correctional counselor at North Kern State Prison from 1993 to 1997 and at the California Correctional Institution from 1991 to 1993.

This position does not require Senate confirmation and the compensation is $135,828. Mr. Sherman is a Republican.
