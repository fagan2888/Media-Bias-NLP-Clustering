CMS Pressed On 'Creative Accounting' At ACA Co-Ops

Law360, New York (November 2, 2015, 9:33 PM ET) -- Two Senate committee leaders on Monday questioned whether the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services is allowing “creative accounting” to help co-ops established under the Affordable Care Act appear more profitable.



As of Monday, 11 such co-ops have announced their intention to shut down, according to a letter from Senate Finance Committee Chairman Orrin Hatch, R-Utah, and Senate Health, Education, Labor and Pensions Committee Chairman Lamar Alexander, R-Tenn., who said they are now questioning all co-ops’ financial viability and accounting methods.
