From self-driving cars to robot-human relations, the topics at a recent MIT conference painted a tantalizing look at the emerging ‘second machine age.’ But widespread adoption may take a while, says Guest Columnist Irving Wladawsky-Berger. “Such times will arrive when the no-longer-so-amazing technologies and our human societies have jointly co-evolved and adapted to each other, as was the case with electricity and cars in the past, and with the Internet and smartphones more recently.”
