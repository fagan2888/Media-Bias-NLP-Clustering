Like many New Yorkers, my experience buying real estate is limited to playing Monopoly—the classic board game based on the streets of Atlantic City. I may never own a Brooklyn Heights brownstone, but I've bought many a home on St. James Place. Which got me thinking: Could I buy a place in the real-life Atlantic City? With a median home price of $74,000, it may be the cheapest beach town within a three-hour drive of Manhattan. And never mind the low-rent squares just past "Go"—in this struggling casino mecca, even...
