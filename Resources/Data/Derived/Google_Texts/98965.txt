TOKYO—A division among Japan’s central bankers over the need for fiscal overhauls came to light Thursday, with the Bank of Japan’s governor and his deputy differing on the degree of the impact of a sales tax increase in 2014.

Speaking at a parliamentary session, BOJ Gov. Haruhiko Kuroda said an increase in the sales tax to 8% from 5% in April 2014 has had a “slightly larger than expected impact” on the country’s economy.
