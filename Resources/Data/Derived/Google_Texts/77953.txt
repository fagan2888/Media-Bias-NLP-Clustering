Hot, dry weather should prevail through most of northeastern Colorado through the day Friday, with the exception of the Julesburg area along the Nebraska border, which could see thunderstorms.

In Denver skies are mostly sunny Friday and the high temperature should hit about 90 degrees, according to the National Weather Service.

Any thunderstorms developing Friday afternoon will likely be over the far eastern plains, east of a line from Sterling to Akron, along the Nebraska and Kansas borders.

Friday night in Denver skies will be mostly clear and the low temperature should drop to about 56 degrees, the weather service reports.

A cold front should move through northeastern Colorado Saturday, cooling off afternoon weather and bringing an increased chance of thunderstorms.

On Saturday in Denver, scattered showers and thunderstorms are expected, mostly after 3 p.m., the weather service said, with the high hitting 82 degrees.

Stormy weather Saturday afternoon north and northeast of Denver could be severe, with large hail, strong winds and heavy bursts of rain.

Storms over the foothills and mountains Saturday should be less severe than the weather over the plains.

Weather in northeastern Colorado should be drying out Sunday.
