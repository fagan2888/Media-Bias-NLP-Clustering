Thousands of negotiators from more than 190 nations were scheduled to meet in Paris from Nov. 30 to Dec. 11, where they hoped to broker an international agreement to rein-in greenhouse gas emissions and slow climate change. While the anticipated accord won’t be enough to halt global warming on its own, leaders hope that by capturing the international political will to act, it will serve as a springboard to larger progress in the months and years ahead – and in so doing, perhaps become one of the most significant climate agreements in history.
