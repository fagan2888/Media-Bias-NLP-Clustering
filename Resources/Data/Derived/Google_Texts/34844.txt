Maury County will be represented in the TSSAA State Cross Country competition by the Spring Hill High School girls team plus a pair of runners from Columbia Central.

The Lady Raiders, coached by Jeremy Paisley, repeated as champions of the Region 6-A/AA championships. They will run at 10 a.m. Saturday at the Steeplechase Course in Percy Warner Park.

Three of the Spring Hill runners placed in the top 10 as Hannah Highland was fourth, Lizzeth Castro sixth and Casey Thyen was eighth.

Also receiving a medal for placing 16th was Akela Robinson.

Some other area schools were represented in the region event including Summertown and Santa Fe.

Freeman, Quinn qualify

Two Columbia Central High School Cross Country runners qualified for the state championship meet with top 10 finishes at the Region 4-AAA meet held Thursday at Smyrna.

Sophomore Tommy Quinn qualified with an eighth-place finish in the boys division. Junior Micaela Freeman was seventh in the girls division. The top 10 runners in each division qualify for state.

Senior Clara McMillan finished 12th in the girls division, earning a trophy for her efforts.

The performances of Freeman and McMillan lead the girls team to a fourth place finish among the nine girls teams entered. The boys finished sixth out of 13 teams.

Oakland High School won the boys division, followed by Siegel and Riverdale. Blackman and LaVergne high schools were the other two teams to finish ahead of the Central boys.

In the girls division, Siegel, Blackman and Riverdale finished ahead of the Central girls. In addition to the individual qualifiers, the top three teams in each division qualified for state.

Both Quinn and Freeman ran personal record times. Quinn broke the 17-minute barrier with a 16:58. Freeman ran a personal record of 20:08.

Senior Will Riley was the No. 2 runner for the Lions, clocking a 19:06 for a 37th-place finish. The next three places on the team went to sophomores. Chase Rowe was next at 42nd with a 19:44. Adam Centeno edged Beau Fisher by a fraction of a second for 45th place 19:56.50.

Junior Kendall Harville was 56th with a 21:02. Senior Jack Chance was 73rd with a 22:52. There were 88 runners in the boys race.

Deondra Thomas posted a 25th-place finish for the girls with a 23:37. Next was senior April Ackley at 34th with a 24:48. Freshman Haley Brewer was 44th with a 25:55, followed by another freshman, Emily Morgan, 46th with a 26:28. Senior Jessica Thompson was 53rd with a 27:14. There were 70 girls in the race.

Junior David Perryman won a medal with a 12th place finish in the boys junior varsity race. His time was 18:31. Senior Seth Butler was 79th with a 28:27. In the girls JV race, Chloe Harville finished 16th with a 28:41.

The state meet will be at the Steeplechase course at Percy Warner Park in Nashville on Saturday, Nov. 1. The Class AAA girls will run at 1:40 p.m. and the boys at 2:20 p.m.
