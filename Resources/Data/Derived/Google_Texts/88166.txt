‘The Moon blew up without warning and for no apparent reason.” It’s a great opening sentence, if not quite without precedent—see Larry Niven’s “Inconstant Moon,” from 1973. Sci-fi fans might be able to guess what happens next in Neal Stephenson’s “Seveneves” (Morrow, 867 pages, $35). The Moon-fragments will grind each other down till the Earth has a set of rings like Saturn. Meanwhile, a “hard rain” of burning meteorites will eliminate all life on the planet.
