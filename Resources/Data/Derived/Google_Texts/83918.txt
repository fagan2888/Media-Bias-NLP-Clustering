On Jan. 29, 1855, the British House of Commons voted to establish a select committee to look into the government's conduct of the Crimean War. The prime minister, the Earl of Aberdeen, regarded this as a vote of no confidence and immediately resigned. It was perhaps the first time in history that a government had fallen for a reason other than an election, royal displeasure or a coup.
