Rapid City, SD

A woman has died from drowning in Rapid Creek Saturday morning.

Rapid City Police responded to a report of a drowning in Rapid Creek near Steele Avenue and Omaha Street just before 7 a.m. Saturday.

When police arrived, they found an unresponsive woman who had been pulled out of the water and onto the bank of the creek. She was pronounced dead at the scene.

Police believe alcohol was likely a factor in the drowning. The woman's name has not been released pending notification of family. Authorities are continuing to investigate the circumstances of her death.

Rapid City law enforcement wants to remind people that the water in Rapid Creek is still very high and dangerous to those who get near it. Swimming in the creek is strongly discouraged at this time.
