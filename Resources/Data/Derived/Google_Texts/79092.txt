A University of Kansas professor was killed in an accident that shut down a stretch of K-10 near Lawrence for several hours.

Kansas Highway Patrol officials say Alexander Tsiovkh, 63, of Lawrence, was driving westbound on K-10 around 4 p.m. Tuesday afternoon when he crossed left of center and struck an oncoming tractor trailer.

Tsiovkh died at the scene. The other driver was treated at a nearby hospital.

Tsiovkh was as associate professor at KU's Center for Russian, East European and Eurasian studies.

The highway was closed until after midnight Wednesday morning. The accident remains under investigation.
