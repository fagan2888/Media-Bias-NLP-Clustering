A 60-year-old township man was killed Tuesday night while crossing Route 33, police said.

The man, whose name was not released, died on impact when he was hit by a 2009 Jeep heading eastbound on Route 33 near Woodward Road, according to a news release from the Manalapan Lt. Edward Niesz.

Police were call at 10:11 p.m. to the scene of the motor vehicle accident, according to the release. The driver, a Matawan woman, said the man was standing in the right lane against the flow of traffic with dark clothes on and that she was unable to avoid him.

He was taken to Centra-State Medical Center in Freehold, where he was pronounced dead at 11 p.m.

That section of Route 33 was closed for nearly four hours as investigators from the Manalapan Township Police Department and the Monmouth County Serious Collision Analysis Response Team investigated the scene, according to the release. Monmouth County Prosecutor's Office Agent Reginald Grant, MONOC paramedics, Englishtown/Manalapan First Aid, Manalapan Fire Co. #1, New Jersey State Police and the New Jersey Department of Transportation also assisted.

The death remains under investigation, with Patrolman Dennis O'Brien as the lead investigator. Police ask that anyone who saw the death contact O'Brien at 732-446-3400.
