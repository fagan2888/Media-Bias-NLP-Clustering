A musical legend has died in Panama City.

World famous jazz clarinetist Buddy DeFranco died in a Panama City hospital on Christmas Eve. He was 91 years old.

Defranco performed for 75 years of his life. He performed with Tommy Dorsey, Count Basie, Gene Krupa, and Charlie Barnett.

He was featured in solo recordings with musical greats like Frank Sinatra, Art Tatum, Ella Fitzgerald, and Tony Bennett.

DeFranco is survived by his wife, Joyce, and son, Chad.
