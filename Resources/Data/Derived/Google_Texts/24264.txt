Our brethren at the Wall Street Journal addressed Monday what is becoming a familiar subject — the diverging implementation of President Obama’s health-care overhaul that each state will experience.

While the report is useful, we thought there was a handy accessory that we could share with our readers.

Included in the report is a graphic that points out which states have their own exchanges (less than a third) and how many will rely entirely on the federal government to run their exchange (roughly half). It also looks at how many states will expand their Medicaid programs as part of Obamacare (slightly less than half).
