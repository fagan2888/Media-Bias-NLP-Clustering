In 2011, when new U.S. office development was at one of its lowest points in decades, Skanska AB took a risky step: The developer started construction on an approximately $70 million office building in Cambridge, Mass., before it had signed any tenants.

The bet paid off. In December, Skanska said it sold the 123,000 square-foot building for $94.5 million to Alexandria Real Estate Equities Inc., more than 80% leased.
