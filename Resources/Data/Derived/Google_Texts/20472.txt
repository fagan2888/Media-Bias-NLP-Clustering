UNDATED (WSAU-Wheeler News) Federal health officials say almost 184,000 Wisconsinites were getting their health insurance through the Obamacare exchanges as of June 30th. New figures from the Department of Health and Human Services shows that about 90-percent of Wisconsin enrollees were getting tax credits to offset their costs. The average credit is $312-a month.

Most of the Wisconsin residents in the Affordable Care Act are enrolled in the "silver" coverage level, which pays an average of 70-percent of a person's costs. The second-largest group is in the "bronze" level, which pays around 60-percent of consumer costs.

Nationally, almost 10-million people were enrolled in Obamacare plans as of the end of June. 84-percent were getting tax credits.
