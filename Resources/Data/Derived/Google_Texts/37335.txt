Multiple earthquakes struck Oklahoma.

According to the U.S. Geological Survey, at 2:16 a.m. Thursday, a 3.0 magnitude earthquake originated 11 miles northeast of Enid. It was a little less than two miles deep.

At 6:24 a.m., another 3.0 magnitude earthquake was recorded 13 miles west of Perry and 21 miles east, southeast of Enid. It was about three miles deep.

At 7:56 a.m., a 3.1 magnitude earthquake was recorded 19 miles north, northwest of Fairview, and 40 miles east of Woodward. It was about three miles deep.

At 10:46 a.m., another 3.0 magnitude earthquake was recorded 21 miles northwest of Fairview and 34 miles east of Woodward. It was about six miles deep.

At 5:42 p.m., a 3.1 magnitude earthquake was recorded 13 miles east of Waukomis, 14 miles east, southeast of Enid, and 30 miles north, northwest of Guthrie.

At 5:47 p.m., a 3.0 magnitude earthquake was recorded 14 miles east of Woodward, 14 miles east, southeast of Enid, and 30 miles north, northwest of Guthrie.

No damage or injuries were immediately reported.
