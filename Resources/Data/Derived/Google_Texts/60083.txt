Early in the 1950s, when the United Nations campus was nearing completion, Richard Rodgers and Oscar Hammerstein II created a musical that illustrates how the whole world is interconnected, showing how a 19th-century king of Siam with a household full of slaves might be inspired by a U.S. president grappling with the issue of slavery himself. That musical is “The King and I,” which is now in previews on Broadway and opens on April 16 in its first revival there since the 1990s.
