The Hyatt Regency Denver in downtown was evacuated early Saturday morning after a suspicious vehicle in the area prompted a bomb squad response.

The Denver Police Department’s bomb squad responded to 14th and Welton streets around 11 p.m. on Friday.

Valet workers notified police about a “credible threat” associated with a vehicle, said Denver police spokeswoman Raquel Lopez.

At 3:45 a.m. on Saturday, police tweeted the incident was still ongoing and the hotel was being evacuated.

At 5:15 a.m., police said the suspicious vehicle had been cleared and hotel guests were allowed back inside.

Police said the incident prompted an ongoing investigation.

As of 9 a.m., there were no further details available.
