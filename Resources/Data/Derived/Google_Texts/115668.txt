Add another data point to a lackluster holiday season.

ShopperTrak, which gets its data from 60,000 traffic-tracking devices installed at large retailers, estimated that sales during the crucial year-end period rose by 2.7% from the same period a year before. While that was better than the 2.4% increase ShopperTrak projected ahead of the holidays, it was the weakest showing since 2009, when sales fell 0.8%.
