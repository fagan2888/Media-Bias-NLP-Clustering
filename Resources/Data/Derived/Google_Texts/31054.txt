The United States established full diplomatic relations with the Holy See in 1984, after President Ronald Reagan, with bipartisan support, persuaded Congress to join more than 150 other countries in maintaining a diplomatic mission to the Vatican. That decision proved strategically crucial, as President Reagan and Pope John Paul II worked along parallel tracks to accelerate the revolutions of 1989 and the eventual collapse of the Soviet Union.
