On Friday, the Fort Myers Police Department served three search warrants in the city that led to the arrests of five people on drug-related charges.

The first warrant was served at 4750 Nottingham Drive as a result of a complaint that methamphetamine was being manufactured in the home.

During the search, police found 10 grams of meth and all the chemicals and items used to produce and manufacture meth.

Keith Schippers was arrested for possession of methamphetamine, possession of chemicals with the intent to manufacture methamphetamine and possession of drug paraphernalia.

The second warrant was served at 2243 Henderson Avenue. The warrant was issued due to a Fort Myers police investigation of several people selling cocaine and heroin from the residence.

During the search, police found a pill vial of crack cocaine and 7.6 grams of heroin packaged for distribution.

Jamaine Bursey was arrested form possession of cocaine with the intent to sell within 1,000 feet of a school and trafficking in heroin. Kavondra Dubose was arrested for possession of cocaine and possession of drug paraphernalia. The investigation remains active.

The third warrant was served at 3319 Handy Court. The warrant was issued after an investigation of several people selling cocaine and heroin at the residence.

Police found eight grams of crack cocaine, three grams of marijuana and drug paraphernalia.

Michael Jones was arrested for possession of cocaine with the intent to sell within 1,000 feet of a school and possession of marijuana under 20 grams. Broderick Jackson was also arrested for possession of cocaine with the intent to sell within 1,000 feet of a school and possession of marijuana under 20 grams. This investigation remains active.
