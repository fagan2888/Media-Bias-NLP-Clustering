Sholem Aleichem, perhaps the greatest, certainly the most popular, Yiddish writer, was born Sholem Rabinovich in the small town of Pereyaslav, near Kiev, in 1859, two years before Czar Alexander II liberated Russia's millions of serfs and made it seem possible that his country might evolve into a liberal European state. Ivan Turgenev, as humane a novelist as ever caught hell from all sides, said that the Russian peasant was "the worst conservative of all, caring nothing for liberal ideas."
