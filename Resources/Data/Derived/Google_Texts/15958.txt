David Cameron and Alexis Tsipras are miles apart politically, but they share more in common than either may care to admit.

Both the U.K. and Greek prime ministers took office after elections in which their parties secured just 37% of the vote. Both claim a strong mandate to reform their country’s relationship with the European Union—and boast that their real aim is to reform the EU itself.
