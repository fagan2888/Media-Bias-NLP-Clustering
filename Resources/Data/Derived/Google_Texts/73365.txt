Trey Edmunds ran for four touchdowns, the first three set up by Miami special-teams miscues, and visiting Virginia Tech knocked off the 14th-ranked Hurricanes, 42-24, on Saturday night.

Edmunds had scoring runs of 10, 2, 4 and 1 yards for the Hokies (7-3, 4-2 Atlantic Coast Conference). Logan Thomas completed 25 of 31 passes for 366 yards, giving him consecutive games of 300 yards or more for the first time in his Virginia Tech career.

Stephen Morris completed 16 of 29 passes for 324 yards for Miami (7-2, 3-2), which lost its second straight.

PITTSBURGH 28, NOTRE DAME 21 James Conner ran for two short touchdowns, including the go-ahead score with 9 minutes 36 seconds remaining, as host Pittsburgh upset No. 24 Notre Dame.

Tom Savage passed for 243 yards and 2 scores to Devin Street as the Panthers (5-4) took advantage of some sloppy play by the Fighting Irish (7-3) to end Notre Dame’s four-game winning streak.

ARIZONA STATE 20, UTAH 19 Taylor Kelly ran for a touchdown and threw for another in the fourth quarter, and No. 23 Arizona State (7-2, 5-1 Pacific-12) came from behind to beat host Utah (4-5, 1-5).

Arizona State’s all-American defensive tackle, Will Sutton, sealed the victory with an interception at the Utah 25.

Kelly finished 19 of 31 for 144 yards, and Marion Grice rushed 20 times for 136 yards.

U.C.F. 19, HOUSTON 14 William Stanback scored two touchdowns and No. 19-ranked Central Florida held off a late Houston threat to hold on to first place in the American Athletic Conference.

Storm Johnson added 127 yards rushing for the Golden Knights (7-1, 4-0), which has the inside track to the league’s automatic Bowl Championship Series bid with victories against the Cougars (7-2, 4-1) and Louisville.

AUBURN 55, TENNESSEE 23 Nick Marshall gained 214 of the Tigers’ 444 rushing yards, and seventh-ranked Auburn (9-1, 5-1 Southeastern Conference) scored on a punt return and a kickoff return in a victory at Tennessee (4-6, 1-5), which lost its third straight.

Marshall ran for two touchdowns on just 14 carries and threw for a third score.

MISSOURI 48, KENTUCKY 17 Maty Mauk threw five touchdown passes, four to Dorial Green-Beckham, and No. 9 Missouri (9-1, 5-1 SEC) cruised past host Kentucky (2-7, 0-5).

Making his fourth consecutive start in place of the injured James Franklin, Mauk completed 17 of 28 attempts for 203 yards.

TEXAS A&M 51, MISSISSIPPI ST. 41 Johnny Manziel threw for 446 yards and tied a career high with five touchdown passes to lead No. 11 Texas A&M (8-2, 4-2 SEC) over Mississippi State (4-5, 1-4) in what may have been his final home game.

WISCONSIN 27, B.Y.U. 17 James White ran for two touchdowns and caught a pass for another score, and No. 21 Wisconsin (7-2) stalled the fast-paced offense of visiting Brigham Young (6-3).

White finished with 147 yards rushing.

OKLAHOMA STATE 42, KANSAS 6 Clint Chelf completed 19 of 37 passes for 265 yards and 3 touchdowns to lead No. 15 Oklahoma State (8-1, 5-1 Big 12) past visiting Kansas (2-7, 0-6).

Desmond Roland added 44 yards and a touchdown on 16 rushes for the Cowboys.

KANSAS STATE 49, TEXAS TECH 26 Jake Waters and Daniel Sams each ran for two touchdowns, and Waters passed for another score as Kansas State (5-4, 3-3 Big 12) earned a victory at No. 25 Texas Tech (7-3, 4-3).

John Hubert, who scored on a 63-yard run early in the game, finished with 157 yards on 23 carries to lead the Wildcats.

FLORIDA ST. 59, WAKE FOREST 3 Jameis Winston threw two touchdown passes, and No. 3 Florida State (9-0, 7-0 A.C.C.) tied a team record with six interceptions in a rout of host Wake Forest (4-6, 2-5). The Seminoles clinched a berth in the conference championship game.

Florida State forced seven turnovers and broke the stadium record of 56 points.
