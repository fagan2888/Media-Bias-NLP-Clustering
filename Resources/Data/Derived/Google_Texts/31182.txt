LEXINGTON, Ky.—A Kentucky county clerk has appealed a judge’s decision to put her in jail for refusing to issue marriage licenses to same-sex couples.

Attorneys for Rowan County Clerk Kim Davis officially appealed the ruling to the 6th Circuit U.S. Court of Appeals on Sunday. The three-page motion doesn’t include arguments as to why Ms. Davis should be released but amends Ms. Davis’ earlier appeal of the judge’s order.
