Actress and TV personality Betty White turns 94 on Sunday.

She was born in Oak Park, IL, on Jan. 17, 1922.

In a career that has spanned more than 75 years, she has received eight Emmy awards, three American Comedy Awards, three Screen Actors Guild awards, and a Grammy.

White is regarded as a pioneer of television.

She is known for her roles as Sue Ann Nivens on The Mary Tyler Moore Show in the 1970s and Rose Nylund on The Golden Girls.

Dubbed the “First Lady of Game Shows,” she was also a regular guest on many game shows, like Match Game, The $25,000 Pyramid and Password.

She starred as Elka Ostrovsky in the TV Land sitcom Hot in Cleveland.

In 2010, she became the oldest person to guest-host Saturday Night Live, a performance which earned her a Primetime Emmy Award.
