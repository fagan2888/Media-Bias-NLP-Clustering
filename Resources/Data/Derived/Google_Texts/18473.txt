WASHINGTON—When 47 Republican senators sent a letter last month to Iran’s leaders panning U.S.-led nuclear talks, Sen. Bob Corker (R., Tenn.) didn’t even consider signing it.

The chairman of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee had his own plans: He didn’t want to risk undercutting bipartisan support for his legislation requiring President Barack Obama to submit any final deal with Iran to Congress for its review.
