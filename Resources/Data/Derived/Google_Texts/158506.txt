THE CERTIFICATE OF MERITORIOUS SERVICE READS: Military Working Dog Bengus, M085, distinguished himself as a patrol and drug detection dog, 22nd Security Forces Squadron, 22nd Mission Support Group, 2nd Air Refueling Wing, McConnell Air Force Base Kansas from 20 November 2008 to 1 February 2016. During this period, Military Working Dog Bengus partnered with five different handlers to support operations at home and abroad, dedicating 630 hours to drug detection Additionally, Bengus provided 6,250 sweeps of drug detection securing the air force’s largest tanker refueler fleet and fifteen thousand McConnell Air Force Base airmen, civilians, and dependents. Furthermore, Military Working Dog Bengus deployed one time to Afghanistan in support of Operation Enduring Freedom, where he had three drug finds. one of those finds totaled 293 grams that included black tar heroin, meth, hash, and opium laced chewing dip. Finally, Military Working Dog Bengus executed more than 800 random antiterrorism measures; safeguarding 15,000 military and civilian personnel and $6 billion in vital Air Force assets. the distinctive accomplishments of Military Working Dog Bengus reflect admirably upon the Department of Defense’s Military Working Dog Program. 22nd Security Forces Squadron military working dog Bengus retired after 63 dog years of active military service.

On Friday, a formal retirement ceremony was held for Bengus to highlight his career and accomplishments including multiple deployments protecting his fellow Airmen.

During retirement, Bengus is being adopted by his handler.
