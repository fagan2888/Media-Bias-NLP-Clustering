The designers of ObamaCare were politically clever in at least one respect: They wrote the so-called Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act so that its most burdensome provisions wouldn’t take effect for years. Although Democratic members of Congress paid a heavy price anyway, President Obama was able to win re-election (with the help, to be sure, of the Internal Revenue Service). That means his “signature achievement” is largely secure against repeal efforts until 2017.
