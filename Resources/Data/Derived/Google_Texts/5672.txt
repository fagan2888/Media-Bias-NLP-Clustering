Hillary Clinton's former campaign research director tells The Hill that the second-time White House contender will try to "crack the code" in Iowa to avoid a repeat of her third-place finish in the early voting state.

"I think that she's really going to try to ... crack the code of how do you make Iowans feel like you really care about their vote and are paying attention," Judd Legum, the editor-in-chief of ThinkProgress, said in an interview on Monday.

Legum explained that Clinton went all out in 2008 to win the nation's first presidential caucus state, but only came in third.
