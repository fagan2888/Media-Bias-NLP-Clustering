Thousands of people are expected to attend the 34th Annual Wichita Asian Festival on Saturday, October 25, 5:30-10 p.m., at Century II Convention Hall.

Volunteers of the Wichita Asian Association have produced the event since 1981.

The annual festival showcases cultural performances, authentic food from 14 countries, and the Miss Asian Festival Scholarship Pageant.

Vendors in 48 food and arts booths will sell Asian food and merchandise. The main stage features performances such as a martial arts demonstration, folk music, and traditional dances from seven Asian countries.

One of the highlights of the festival is the Miss Asian Festival Scholarship Pageant, which starts at 7:30 p.m. Eight contestants will compete in the pageant and represent the country of their cultural heritage.

Admission is free but donations are encouraged.
