From this morning’s LBO Wire:

Carlyle Group is acquiring a majority stake in insurance broker JenCap Holdings LLC and the assets of Wholesale Trading Co-Op Insurance Services.

The Washington, D.C., firm said it would fund equity for the deal out of Carlyle Global Financial Services Partners II LP. The vehicle had a final close in 2014 at $1 billion.

More stories available to LBO Wire subscribers:

Audax Group agreed to recapitalize Gastro Health PL, a gastroenterology physician practice management company…Wellnext LLC, a nutritional supplement provider owned by WM Partners, said it will acquire Natural Dynamics LLC, which does business as Natural Vitality…And Bustle, which operates an online news site catering to women, raised $11.5 million in additional funding led by Saban Capital Group.

Elsewhere:

Sanjeev Mehra, a co-founder of Goldman Sachs Group’s private equity arm, will retire this year after nearly 30 years with the firm, writes Justin Baer for The Wall Street Journal.

Speaking of departures, David Arcauz, a partner in the European primary team at Adams Street Partners, left the firm after six years, reports Yolanda Bobeldijk for U.K. sister publication Financial News (subscription required).

Cerberus Capital Management, which is leading a group of investors that own the West Coast’s Albertsons grocery chain, received antitrust clearance to bid on the profitable core of the Haggen grocery chain, which is going on the bankruptcy auction block next week, reports Peg Brickley for the Dow Jones Daily Bankruptcy Review.

Brazilian firm Aqua Capital raised $188 million for its second fund, New York Times DealBook reports.
