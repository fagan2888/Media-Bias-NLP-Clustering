DOJ Antitrust Chief Airs Doubts On Health Insurer Tie-Ups

Law360, New York (November 13, 2015, 6:57 PM ET) -- The U.S. Department of Justice’s top antitrust enforcer on Friday voiced skepticism about the benefits of consolidation in the health insurance industry, a potential red flag for proposed megamergers among leading insurers.



Assistant Attorney General Bill Baer, head of the DOJ’s Antitrust Division, made the comments at a Yale Law School conference on competition in the health care industry, according to prepared remarks released by the department.
