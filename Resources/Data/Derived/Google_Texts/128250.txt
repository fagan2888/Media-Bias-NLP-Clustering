Kaiser Health News recently used Dallas County as a model for how taxpayers are overpaying for uncompensated healthcare because of their state’s refusal to expand Medicaid or agree on an alternative.

As the nonprofit notes, Texas and the 20 other states that haven’t expanded the program under the Affordable Care Act are forcing residents not only to pay a portion of the taxes for the uninsured, but also the cost of Medicaid expansion in the states that have chosen to do so. Last year, Dallas County property owners paid $467 million in taxes for care provided at Parkland Health & Hospital System.
