The words 'Western Front' bring indelible images to mind: mud-streaked, steel-helmeted figures fighting for survival in a hellish landscape disfigured by snaking trenches and corpse-filled shell holes.

While not disputing these brutal realities, evoked so powerfully by such poets as Siegfried Sassoon and Wilfred Owen, in "Behind the Front" Canadian historian Craig Gibson reminds us that the mud, blood and sacrifice of the trenches was just one aspect of the British experience of the Western Front.
