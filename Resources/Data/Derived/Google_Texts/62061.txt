Today in Obama:

While we’re waiting for President Barack Obama to drop an executive order reforming immigration without Congressional approval, the New York Times reminds us that the president once declared he couldn’t reform immigration without Congressional approval.

With Obama in meetings all day, he left it to his chief of staff, John Podesta, to wave the veto pen at Congress ahead of an anticipated vote over the Keystone XL pipeline, even though Democrats are one vote shy of the 60 they need to pass the bill.
