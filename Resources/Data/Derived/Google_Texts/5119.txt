A winter weather advisory has been issued for parts of southern Wisconsin on Monday, with 3 to 5 inches of blowing snow beginning in the afternoon, according to the National Weather Service in Sullivan.

The advisory is in effect from 1 p.m. Monday to 6 a.m. Tuesday for Dodge, Jefferson, Walworth, Waukesha and Washington counties.

It will be a big change from Sunday, when the high rose to nearly 50 degrees after a week of below-freezing temperatures.

Hazardous weather, with fog and patchy dense fog Sunday night followed by light snow on Monday, is forecast for the following counties: Marquette, Green Lake, Fond du Lac, Sheboygan, Sauk, Columbia, Dodge, Washington, Ozaukee, Iowa, Dane, Jefferson, Waukesha, Milwaukee, Lafayette, Green, Rock, Walworth, Racine and Kenosha.

In the Milwaukee area, the forecast calls for rain before 1 p.m. on Monday and a rain/snow mix between 1 and 2 p.m. when it's expected to switch over to snow. Periods of blowing snow are likely before midnight with a low Monday night around 22 degrees.

Snow accumulations on Monday are predicted at less than an inch.
