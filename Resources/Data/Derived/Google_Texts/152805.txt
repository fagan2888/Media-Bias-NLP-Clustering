The holiday honoring the Rev. Martin Luther King Jr. is Jan. 20. Here’s a sampling of related activities around the Dallas area:

GARDERE MLK JR. ORATORY COMPETITION (FREE)

In this 22nd annual oratory competition in Dallas, held concurrently with Martin Luther King Jr. Day celebrations, Dallas and Houston fourth- and fifth-grade elementary school students will deliver original five-minute speeches based on ideals presented in King’s writing and speeches. This year, the students’ topic is: “If Dr. King were speaking at a March on Washington today, what would he say?”

Jan. 17 from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m. at the Majestic Theatre, 1925 Elm St., Dallas. Free. gardere.com.

MARTIN LUTHER KING JR. BIRTHDAY CELEBRATION (FREE)

The city of Dallas and the Martin Luther King Jr. Community Center will present several events as part of the 32nd annual Martin Luther King Jr. Birthday Celebration. This year’s theme is “Freedom, Equality, & Opportunity, Forever!”

The following events are free. 214-670-8418. More event information is available on the website. mlkcelebrationdallas.org.

Candle Lighting Ceremony: The Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity Inc. will host this program and ceremony, which begins Jan. 17 at 7 p.m. at the Martin Luther King Jr. Recreation Center, 2901 Pennsylvania Ave., Dallas.

March/Parade: Begins Jan. 18 at 10 a.m. at Dallas City Hall, 1500 Marilla St. in downtown Dallas, and progresses along a 2.9 mile route from South Ervay Street, south to Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard, and east to Robert B. Cullum Boulevard, ending at the Fair Park entrance.

MLK Jr. Super Saturday Festival: Activities at this health and information fair will include Affordable Care Act information and enrollment. Jan. 18 from 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. at the Martin Luther King Jr. Recreation Center, 2901 Pennsylvania Ave., Dallas.

DR. MARTIN LUTHER KING JR. PARADE AND MARCH IN GARLAND (FREE)

The NAACP’s Garland unit will host its 25th annual Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. Parade and March, with the theme “Preparing Our Youth for Success in the 21st Century.” Attorney Gary Bledsoe, Texas State NAACP president, and Linda Lydia, Texas State NAACP secretary, will be this year’s grand marshals for the parade, which will begin near Embree Park on Dairy Road and end at the Granville Arts Center. Immediately after the parade, the celebration will continue with an MLK Commemorative Program in the Granville Arts Center.

Jan. 18, beginning with the parade and march at 10 a.m. and continuing with an MLK Commemorative Program at the Granville Arts Center’s Brownlee Auditorium, 300 N. Fifth St., in downtown Garland. Free. 972-381-5044, voice box #5. garlandtxnaacp.org.

‘BLACK MUSIC & THE CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENT CONCERT’

The Black Academy of Arts and Letters presents this 31st annual tribute concert, which will be held this year at the Meyerson Symphony Center in downtown Dallas. The televised concert honors the philosophy of King and the civil rights movement of the 1960s through film, music, dance and narration. The event will feature Grammy award winner Peabo Bryson.

Jan. 19 at 7:30 p.m. at the Meyerson Symphony Center, 2301 Flora St., Dallas. Admission is $15-$30, available by calling TBAAL’s box office at 214-743-2400 or through Ticketmaster. tbaal.org.

ELITE NEWS MARTIN LUTHER KING JR. DAY PARADE (FREE)

The 28th annual parade will include bands, floats and community groups. It will be followed by a festival in Fair Park’s Tower Building featuring a children’s area, live entertainment, food vendors and more.

The parade begins Jan. 20 at 10 a.m. at Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard and Lamar Street, Dallas, and proceeds to Fair Park. Festival doors will be open from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Viewing the parade and festival admission are free. dfwelitenews.com.
