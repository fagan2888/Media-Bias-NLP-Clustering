DOJ Joins FCA Suit Over 60-Day Overpayment Rule

Law360, New York (June 27, 2014, 6:21 PM ET) -- The U.S. Department of Justice on Friday joined a whistleblower’s False Claims Act suit accusing New York's Mount Sinai Health System of failing to return hundreds of Medicaid overpayments within 60 days of identifying them, one of the first examples of litigation involving an important Affordable Care Act provision.



In its intervening complaint, the DOJ asserted that Continuum Health Partners Inc. — now a part of Mount Sinai — improperly billed Medicaid beginning in 2009 on behalf of Beth Israel Medical Center and St. Luke's-Roosevelt Hospital...
