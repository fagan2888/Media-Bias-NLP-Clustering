U.S. gun sales are slowing from their recent torrid pace.

Smith & Wesson Holding Corp. said late Tuesday that its profit in the three months ended Oct. 31 fell 20% from the year-earlier quarter as sales rose just 2%. That follows rival Remington Outdoor Co.'s forecast a day earlier for sales growth between about 34% and 37% for 2013, slowing from the 51% surge in the first nine months of this year.
