Florida still leads the nation with nearly 1.4 million people selecting a plan in healthcare.gov, according to the latest government data released on Wednesday.

Almost 168,000 people in Orlando-Kissimmee-Sanford area have selected a plan, compared with150,000 in Tampa-St. Petersburg-Clearwater and 662,000 in Miami-Fort Lauderdale-West Palm Beach.

Nationwide, 7.75 million people have selected a plan or have been automatically re-enrolled in one, according to the Department of Health and Human Services.

Feb. 15 is the last day to sign up for health insurance in the federal marketplace this year, and for last-minute enrollees, several fairs around Central Florida aim to help with the sign-up process and tax questions.
