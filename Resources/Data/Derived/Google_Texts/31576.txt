Police say a Sloop Creek Drive home was used to manufacture marijuana and narcotics.

William Faiges. Source: Ocean County Jail (Photo: Ocean County Jail)

BERKELEY â€” Police say a Sloop Creek Drive home in the Bayville section of the township was used to prepare and distribute marijuana and other narcotics.

Sloop Creek Drive residents Larry MacTaggart and Jean Brewer were arrested Feb. 23 along with William Faiges of Ocean Gate during a search of the home, Berkeley police said in a news release on Facebook.

Police said they discovered marijuana, paraphernalia related to the sale of narcotics, a digital scale and packaging material.

MacTaggart, 52, was charged with possession of marijuana with the intent to distribute.

Brewer, 57, was charged with possession of a hypodermic syringe.

Faiges, 60, was arrested on an outstanding warrant related to a theft in Berkeley. He was taken to the Ocean County Jail, Toms River, and held in default of $5,000 bail, according to jail records.

MacTaggart and Brewer were charged and released, according to Berkeley police.

"It should be noted that this successful investigation was the result of concerned citizens who reported suspicious activity to police," Berkeley police wrote on the department's Facebook page. "Residents are reminded to call the Berkeley Township Police Department TIP line at 732-341-1132 ext. 611. Tips can remain anonymous."

The Ocean County Regional SWAT and the Ocean County Sheriff's Department K-9 unit assisted.

Photos of MacTaggart and Brewer were not immediately available.
