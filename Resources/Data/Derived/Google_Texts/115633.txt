Two earthquakes were reported Tuesday afternoon in northwest Oklahoma, the U.S. Geological Survey reported.

The first was a 3.6-magnitude earthquake happened at 4:12 p.m. Its epicenter was about 19 miles northwest of Fairview, 34 miles east of Woodward and 96 miles northwest of Oklahoma City.

The second quake was a 3.5 magnitude and it happened at 5:05 p.m. Its epicenter was about 18 miles northwest of Fairview, 36 miles east of Woodward and 95 miles northwest of Oklahoma City.

Both earthquakes were less than four miles deep.

No immediate reports of damage or injury were reported.
