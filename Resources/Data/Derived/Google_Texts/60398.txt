U.S. stocks fell for a third straight session Friday to end the week with steep losses, snapping a six-week winning streak. The weekly losses were biggest in 12 weeks. Friday's losses were driven by a selloff in the retail sector following disappointing earnings from big department stores such as Nordstrom Inc. JWN, -0.45% and J.C. Penney JCP, -3.16% The S&P 500 SPX, -0.02% fell 22.91 points, or 1.1%, to 2,023.06 and recorded a 3.6% loss over the week. The Dow Jones Industrial Average DJIA, -0.13% dropped 202.97 points, or 1.2%, to 17,245.10 and was 3.7% lower on the week. The Nasdaq Composite COMP, +0.14% ended the day down 77.20 points, or 1.5%, at 4,927.88 and lost 4.3% over the week.
