TOM BRAKKE: Yes, the demand for a particular fund or for similar groups of funds can say much about the psychology of investors. Understanding the crowd is an important part of active investing (should you decide to do that rather than taking a passive approach).

However, there isn’t a consistent signal from which you can make specific decisions. At extremes–when money is pouring into a fund/strategy or pouring out of one – the crowd is almost always wrong, so cash flows seem like a contrary indicator, but they can be supportive for long periods of time before the excesses of the crowd become apparent.
