The study of history, for some years, has tacked to and fro between countervailing currents. Whereas popular narratives remain strongly wedded to recounting political and military struggles, in tandem with biographies of dead white men on horseback (to paraphrase a noted historian), many academics continue to "privilege" social and cultural histories devoted to the triumvirate of race, class and gender (religion, not so much).
