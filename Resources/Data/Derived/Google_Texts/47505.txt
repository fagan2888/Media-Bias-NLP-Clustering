Title Loan - a loan lasting up to 12 months that uses a borrower’s vehicle title as collateral.

Payday Loan - a short-term unsecured cash advance up to $500. Borrowers get no grace period and are generally required to repay the loan in two pay periods.

Open-ended line of credit – a loan that offer a grace period of 25 days and no term limit on the debt, but also has no ceiling on interest rates.

Consumer Finance Loan – A loan, which may utilize a car’s title as collateral. Only 36 percent interest can be charged to borrowers looking for amounts below $2,500, but can carry unlimited interest rates over $2,500.
