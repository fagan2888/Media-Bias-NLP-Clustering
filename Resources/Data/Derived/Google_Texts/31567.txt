MILAN—Italian eyewear giant Luxottica SpA saw its second-quarter net profit increase by a quarter, as the weakening euro against the dollar boosted its sales result in the period.

The maker of eyewear for the likes of Giorgio Armani and Chanel said its net profit rose 25% to €295 million, while operating profit stood at €500 million, up 26% on the year.
