ATHENS—Greece's four big, systemic banks will need another €5.8 billion ($8.0 billion) to shore up their fragile balance sheets, the country's central bank said Thursday, in order to cope with a growing mountain of bad loans that have become another painful legacy of Greece's protracted debt crisis.
