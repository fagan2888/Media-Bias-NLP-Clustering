The University of New Mexico football team won back-to-back games for the first time since 2012 as the Lobos beat the Aggies 38-29 on Saturday night at University Stadium.

The Aggies jumped out to a 7-nothing lead on Larry Rose's first of three touchdowns before halftime. UNM quarterback Lamar Jordan evened the scored at seven with a 70 yard touchdown run of his own. The Lobos finished with 401 rushing yards on the game.

New Mexico State took a 26-14 lead into halftime, but the Lobos shut down the Aggies in the second half. UNM's defense held their opponent to just three points and 62 total yards in the final two quarters. The Lobos scored the final 24 points of the game, capped off by a safety in the final two minutes.

New Mexico improves to 3-2 on the year and New Mexico State remains winless four games into the season.
