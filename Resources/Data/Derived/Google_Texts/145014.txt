The Metro State’s men’s basketball team is joining Duke, Arizona and Alabama in the National Invitation Tournament Season Tip-Off.

The Roadrunners are the second-ever Division II team to be invited to the Preseason NIT, which this year also includes the third, Stillman College from Tuscaloosa, Ala. Alabama-Huntsville was the first, in 2012. Metro State will play Rhode Island at 8 p.m. Nov. 18. The game will be available on the TV website WatchESPN. The tournament will be carried on ESPN, ESPN2, ESPN3 and ESPNU.

“We are honored to be only the second Division II team to compete in this prestigious tournament,” Metro State coach Derrick Clark said in a release. “This is a compliment to Mike Dunlap, Bob Hull and all the former players that have helped create the rich tradition of Metro State basketball.”

Last season, the Roadrunners finished 32-3 and reached the Division II national championship game, falling 74-73 to Drury.

The 29th annual tournament will feature Arizona, Duke, Alabama and Rutgers as the top four seeds. Also playing will be fifth see Rhode Island, sixth seed Elon, seventh seed East Carolina and eightth seed Drexel. Rounding out the tournament will be Metro State, Canisius, Fairleigh Dickinson, Georgia State, McNeese State, Norfolk State, UNC Asheville and Stillman.

“Year after year the NIT Season Tip-Off proves to be a showcase for outstanding teams, and this year is no different,” said NIT selection committee chair C.M. Newton.

The first- and second-round doubleheaders will be played Nov. 18 and 19 on the campuses of the four highest-seeded teams, and the winners of the preliminary rounds advance to the semifinals at New York City’s Madison Square Garden on Nov. 27. The consolation and championship games will be played Nov. 29. Denver Post staff and wire services
