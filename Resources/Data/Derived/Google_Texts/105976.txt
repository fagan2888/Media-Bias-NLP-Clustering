Snow is falling in some areas of KELOLAND.

From Aberdeen to Sioux Falls, up to two inches of snow is in the forecast.

Strong winds are also back and will continue through the day.

According to KELOLAND Meteorologist Scot Mundt, Rapid City had a 62 miles per hour gust at 8:47 a.m. Wind advisories are posted for much of KELOLAND, with gusts over 40 mph expected.

The South Dakota Highway Patrol is asking drivers of high-profile vehicles to be careful. Monday morning, the Northern Plains squad had to help when a truck blew over on US 85 north of Buffalo.

Dangerous wind chills will return Monday afternoon through Tuesday morning.
