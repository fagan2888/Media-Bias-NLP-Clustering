JAKARTA, Indonesia—Just over a year ago, Indonesia created what has quickly become, at least for now, the world’s largest national health insurance system. It aimed to give its 250 million citizens a safety net that would encourage them to spend more freely instead of saving for rainy-day medical emergencies, boosting the economy.
