PARIS—France’s antitrust authority on Thursday said it fined a dozen personal and home-care products companies a total of nearly €1 billion, or about $1.23 billion, for anticompetitive practices between 2003 and 2006.

The Autorité de la Concurrence fined companies including Colgate-Palmolive Co., Unilever NV, Procter & Gamble Co. and Reckitt Benckiser Group PLC a total of €345.2 million for collusion, including coordinating price increases for their home-care products sold to supermarkets.
