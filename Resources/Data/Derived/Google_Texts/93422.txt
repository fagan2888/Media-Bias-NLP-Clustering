The American Red Cross opened the latest shelter in Adams County and a new one in East Denver.

A shelter has been set up in Commerce City at the Adams County High School, located at 7200 Quebec Parkway, and another at the Evie Dennis School at 4800 Telluride Drive in Denver.

Thursday morning, the Red Cross met with the Colorado National Guard and community organizations to help staff the centers, according to a news release. The National Guard is working to bring 200 cots and 200 blankets to the shelter in Lyons.

Red Cross workers were not able to reach the shelter this morning, due to impassibility of roads and hazardous wastewater.

About 200 Lyons residents have been evacuated.

The National Guard is also bringing supplies to the Jamestown area, but access to Jamestown remains limited.

A shelter has been set up in Boulder, at the YMCA of Boulder Valley, 2850 Mapleton Ave.

The Red Cross is also assisting at other shelters for flood victims in Boulder County:

• Highlands Presbyterian Camp and Retreat — located at 1306 Business Highway 7 in Allenspark — which is a camp that has pillows, blankets and beds.

• Nederland Community Center, located at 750 Colorado Highway 72 is open.

• The St. Vrain Valley School District Mead High School has been set up as a shelter for Longmont residents. The high school is located at 12750 County Road 7.

•Residents may go to the Memorial Building and Recreation Center, located at 700 Longs Peak Ave in Longmont.

• Niwot High School, , 8989 East Niwot Road, has been set up as an evacuation center.

• Residents may also go to the Erie Community center, located at 450 Powers Street.

Evacuees may take their smaller pets to the Boulder Valley Humane Society, located at 2323 55th Street. Larger animals may be taken to the Boulder County Fairgrounds, located at 9595 Nelson Road in Longmont.

Large animals evacuated from Commerce City are being accepted at the Douglas County Fairgrounds 500 Fairgrounds Drive in Castle Rock.
