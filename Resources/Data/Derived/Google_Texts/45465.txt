Police are searching for a hit-and-run driver who struck a pedestrian Thursday morning in South Amboy. (Photo: ~File photo)

SOUTH AMBOY – Police are investigating a hit-and-run accident that left a pedestrian with a significant injuries early Thursday morning.

At about 3 a.m., a pedestrian, wearing dark clothes, was walking south in the roadway on Route 9 and 35 South in the vicinity of Amboy Motel, when the pedestrian was struck, police said. The impact sheered the passenger side mirror off the car and sent the pedestrian into and over a guard rail. The injured pedestrian was able to make his way to the Amboy Motel, where a concerned citizen called 911. The pedestrian was taken to Robert Wood Johnson University Hospital with paramedics on board, police said.

The vehicle is described as a 2014-2015 black or dark Chevy Impala that is missing its passenger side mirror and could have some other passenger side damage, police said.

The South Amboy Police Department’s Detective Bureau is investigating the incident and is asking the public’s help with locating the vehicle and driver.

Anyone with information is asked to contact the South Amboy Police Department at 732-721-0111 and have them pass the information to the on-call detective or leave a message for Detective Matthew Barcheski at 732-525-5951 or Detective David Keegan at 732-525-5944. Callers can remain anonymous if they wish.
