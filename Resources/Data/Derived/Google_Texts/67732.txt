CMS Databases Increase Qui Tam Risk For Health Providers

Law360, New York (December 11, 2014, 10:27 AM ET) -- In 2014, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services released two new sources of data that are intended to increase transparency related to federal payments to physicians. They also, however, present a risk of being used by whistleblowers and attorneys to support qui tam lawsuits alleging violations of the False Claims Act (FCA), the Anti-Kickback Act or the Physician Self-Referral Law, commonly called the Stark Law.
