OKLAHOMA CITY (AP) - The Oklahoma Senate has given final approval to a bill authorizing the sale of $125 million in bonds to complete repairs and renovations to the nearly 100-year-old state Capitol. The bill approved Thursday on a 30-16 vote is the second phase of the bond issue, bringing the total price tag for overhauling the Capitol to $245 million. The measure now heads to Gov. Mary Fallin, who is expected to sign it.

CHAPMAN, Kan. (AP) - Forecasters say Kansas will see more bad weather as communities clean up from storms that included a tornado on the ground for about 90 minutes. The Storm Prediction Center said there is a "moderate risk" that Kansas on Thursday would have tornadoes, including some significant ones with winds above 136 mph. A strong storm Wednesday churned across the prairie near Chapman, Kansas, for an hour and a-half.

DES MOINES, Iowa (AP) - Six states and the Federal Highway Administration have agreed to make the roadsides along Interstate 35 friendlier for bees and butterflies to help the plant-pollinating insects boost their declining populations. Officials from Minnesota, Iowa, Missouri, Kansas, Oklahoma and Texas and the federal government signed an agreement Thursday to improve the habitat and develop a branding campaign informally naming the interstate the "Monarch Highway."

OKLAHOMA CITY (AP) - More than 100 protesters at the Oklahoma state Capitol have called for the Legislature to scrap a proposed budget deal and designate more state money for public education. Members of the protest group on Thursday marched from a nearby high school and rallied in the Capitol rotunda between the legislative chambers.
