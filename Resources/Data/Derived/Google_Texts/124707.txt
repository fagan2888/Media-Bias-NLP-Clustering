Kansas professor expelled from classroom after tweeting NRA death wish

Following a tweet calling for the deaths of NRA members’ children, University of Kansas David Guth is back at work today, but has been banished from the classroom for the offensive statement. Guth will continue to remain employed at the university, but will be doing as much work away from students as possible. Read more here.

Study: Kansas premiums to spike following Obamacare rollout

A report recently released by the Heritage Foundation compares insurance premiums before and after the expected rollout of Obamacare in the coming weeks and months, and the news isn’t good. Young people in Kansas are set to see the sixth-highest spike in premiums cost nationwide. Read more here.

State education board member pitches private business at Capitol

According to state ethics officials, there wasn’t anything legally wrong with state education board member Steve Roberts decision to pump his tutoring business to visiting high school students, but that didn’t keep him from drawing the ire of his colleagues. Read more here.
